Queen's Park Swizzle

TropicalHerbalCitrusSpicyWoodyGrassy
  • Aged Rum60 ml
  • Fresh Lime Juice30 ml
  • Simple Syrup22 ml
  • Mint Leaves8 leaves
  • Angostura Bitters2 dashes

Preparation

1. Gently Muddle: In a highball glass, gently muddle the mint leaves with the simple syrup. Be careful not to over-muddle, as this can release bitter compounds. 2. Add Ingredients: Add the aged rum, lime juice, and Angostura bitters to the glass. 3. Fill with Crushed Ice: Fill the glass completely with crushed ice. 4. Swizzle: Insert a swizzle stick (or a long spoon) into the glass and swizzle vigorously, using a back-and-forth motion. The goal is to chill and aerate the drink simultaneously, creating a frosty effect. Continue swizzling until the glass is frosted on the outside. 5. Add More Ice: Add more crushed ice to form a cone above the rim of the glass. 6. Garnish: Garnish with a generous sprig of fresh mint. Optionally, dust the mint sprig with powdered sugar.

Garnish

Mint sprig, dusted with powdered sugar (optional)

Sazerac

HerbalSpicyBitterCitrusWoody
  • Rye Whiskey60 ml
  • Absinthe7 ml
  • Sugar Cube1 pc
  • Peychaud's Bitters3 dashes

Preparation

Chill an Old Fashioned glass. In a separate mixing glass, muddle the sugar cube with the Peychaud's Bitters. Add the rye whiskey and ice. Stir well until chilled. Rinse the chilled Old Fashioned glass with the absinthe, coating the inside of the glass, then discard the excess. Strain the whiskey mixture into the absinthe-rinsed glass. Express the oil from the lemon peel over the glass and discard.

Garnish

Lemon peel twist, expressed over the glass and discarded. The oils add aroma and a hint of citrus without adding juice.

Glögg

Flaming Volcano

TropicalFruitySweetSpicy
  • Light Rum60 ml
  • Dark Rum30 ml
  • Brandy30 ml
  • Orgeat Syrup15 ml
  • Pineapple Juice60 ml
  • Orange Juice30 ml
  • Lime Juice15 ml
  • Sugar Cube1 pc

Preparation

1. Combine light rum, dark rum, brandy, pineapple juice, orange juice, lime juice, and orgeat syrup in a shaker with ice. 2. Shake well until chilled. 3. Pineapple wedge, cherry, and a flaming sugar cube soaked in high-proof rum. Ensure sugar cube is placed on a heat-resistant surface within the bowl before ignition.

Quentão

SpicyFruityCitrusWoodySweet
  • Cachaça500 ml
  • Water200 ml
  • Granulated Sugar200 g
  • Fresh Ginger50 g
  • Cinnamon Sticks3 pc
  • Whole Cloves6 pc
  • Orange1 pc
  • Lime1 pc

Preparation

1. Slice the ginger into thin coins. Cut the orange and lime into quarters. 2. In a large pot, combine the water, sugar, ginger, cinnamon sticks, and cloves. 3. Bring the mixture to a boil over medium heat, stirring until the sugar is dissolved. 4. Reduce the heat to low and add the orange and lime pieces. Simmer for about 15-20 minutes to allow the flavors to infuse. 5. Stir in the Cachaça. 6. Heat gently for another 5 minutes, being careful not to boil off the alcohol. (The mixture is supposed to be gently heated). 7. Strain the mixture to remove the solids. 8. Serve hot in mugs or heat-resistant glasses. Garnish as specified.

Garnish

Orange slices studded with cloves and cinnamon sticks.

Cremat

CitrusSpicyFruityNuttyWoodySmoky
  • Rum200 ml
  • Granulated Sugar100 g
  • Lemon Peel1 pc
  • Orange Peel1 pc
  • Coffee Beans5 pc
  • Cinnamon Stick1 pc

Preparation

1. In a heat-resistant earthenware bowl or pot, combine the rum, sugar, lemon peel, orange peel, coffee beans, and cinnamon stick. 2. Stir the mixture gently to dissolve some of the sugar. 3. Carefully heat the rum mixture, either on a stovetop or using a heat source under the bowl. Ensure it doesn't boil. 4. Using a ladle, scoop some of the heated rum and pour it back over the sugar and spices. This helps to dissolve the sugar further and infuse the flavors. 5. Once the sugar is mostly dissolved, carefully ignite the surface of the rum with a long match or lighter. The alcohol fumes will catch fire and burn with a blue flame. 6. Allow the rum to burn for a few minutes, stirring occasionally with a ladle. The longer it burns, the more alcohol evaporates, reducing the overall booziness. 7. Extinguish the flames by covering the bowl with a lid or by carefully adding a small amount of water or coffee. 8. Ladle the Cremat into heat-resistant glasses or mugs, ensuring that each serving contains a portion of the citrus peels and coffee beans. 9. Serve immediately and enjoy warm.

Garnish

Lemon peel, orange peel, coffee beans (3-5), caramelized sugar around the rim (optional)
