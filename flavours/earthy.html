Cocktails with Earthy flavor

Mint Julep

HerbalSweetWoodyEarthyVanilla
  • Bourbon Whiskey60 ml
  • Fresh Mint Leaves10 leaves
  • Simple Syrup10 ml

Preparation

1. Gently muddle mint leaves and simple syrup in a julep cup or sturdy rocks glass. Avoid over-muddling, which can release bitter compounds. 2. Fill the cup completely with crushed ice. 3. Add bourbon. 4. Gently stir until the cup is frosted. 5. Add more crushed ice to form a mound. 6. Garnish with a generous sprig of fresh mint. 7. Optionally, dust with powdered sugar.

Garnish

A generous sprig of fresh mint, lightly slapped to release its aroma. Optionally, dust with powdered sugar.

Ti' Punch

HerbalGrassyCitrusEarthyTropical
  • Rhum Agricole50 ml
  • Lime1 wedge
  • Cane Syrup or Brown Sugar1 barspoon

Preparation

In an old-fashioned glass, gently muddle the lime wedge and cane syrup or brown sugar. Add the rhum agricole. Add one small ice cube (optional, but traditional) and stir briefly to combine, allowing the ice to chill and slightly dilute the drink. The drink is traditionally served without a strainer.

Garnish

Lime wheel or twist. Express the oils over the drink before placing it in the glass.

Oaxaca Old Fashioned

SmokyHerbalSpicyEarthySweet
  • Tequila45 ml
  • Mezcal15 ml
  • Agave Nectar5 ml
  • Angostura Bitters2 dashes

Preparation

Combine tequila, mezcal, agave nectar, and Angostura bitters in a mixing glass. Add ice and stir until well-chilled and properly diluted (about 20-30 seconds). Strain into a rocks glass over a large ice cube. Express the oils from an orange peel over the glass, then drop the peel into the glass.

Garnish

Orange peel, expressed and placed in the glass.

Irish Car Bomb

CoffeeChocolateCreamyBitterEarthyWoody
  • Irish Whiskey30 ml
  • Irish Stout236 ml
  • Irish Cream Liqueur15 ml

Preparation

1. Pour the Irish stout into a pint glass, filling approximately three-quarters full. 2. Carefully layer the Irish cream liqueur on top of the Irish whiskey in a shot glass. 3. Drop the shot glass into the stout and consume immediately.

Garnish

None

Penicillin

SmokySpicyCitrusHerbalSweetEarthy
  • Blended Scotch Whisky60 ml
  • Islay Single Malt Scotch Whisky7 ml
  • Fresh Lemon Juice22 ml
  • Honey-Ginger Syrup22 ml

Preparation

Muddle ginger slices (if using fresh ginger), add blended Scotch, lemon juice, and honey-ginger syrup to a shaker. Add ice and shake well until chilled. Strain into a rocks glass filled with ice. Float the Islay Scotch on top. Garnish with candied ginger and/or a lemon peel.

Garnish

Candied ginger and/or a lemon peel

Lapsang Souchong Martini

SmokyEarthyHerbalCitrusSavory
  • Gin60 ml
  • Dry Vermouth5 ml
  • Lapsang Souchong Tea (strong infusion)3 ml

Preparation

1. Prepare a strong Lapsang Souchong tea infusion (e.g., brew double strength and let cool). 2. Chill a Martini glass. 3. In a mixing glass filled with ice, combine the gin, dry vermouth, and Lapsang Souchong tea infusion. 4. Stir well until thoroughly chilled (approx. 20 seconds). 5. Strain into the chilled Martini glass. 6. Express a lemon twist over the drink and place on the rim.

Garnish

Lemon twist, expressed over the drink and placed on the rim.

Mezcal Margarita

SmokyCitrusFruityEarthySalty
  • Mezcal50 ml
  • Orange Liqueur20 ml
  • Lime Juice25 ml
  • Agave Nectar15 ml

Preparation

1. Prepare the glass: If desired, salt the rim of a chilled rocks glass by running a lime wedge around the rim and dipping it in salt. 2. Combine ingredients: In a shaker, combine the mezcal, lime juice, orange liqueur, and agave nectar. 3. Add ice: Fill the shaker with ice. 4. Shake well: Shake vigorously until well-chilled, about 15-20 seconds. 5. Strain: Strain the mixture into the prepared rocks glass filled with fresh ice. 6. Garnish: Garnish with a lime wedge or wheel.

Garnish

Lime wedge or wheel, optionally a salted rim. Salt application: rub a lime wedge around the rim, then dip into salt.
