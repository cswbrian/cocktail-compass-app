Cocktails with Grassy flavor

Ti' Punch

HerbalGrassyCitrusEarthyTropical
  • Rhum Agricole50 ml
  • Lime1 wedge
  • Cane Syrup or Brown Sugar1 barspoon

Preparation

In an old-fashioned glass, gently muddle the lime wedge and cane syrup or brown sugar. Add the rhum agricole. Add one small ice cube (optional, but traditional) and stir briefly to combine, allowing the ice to chill and slightly dilute the drink. The drink is traditionally served without a strainer.

Garnish

Lime wheel or twist. Express the oils over the drink before placing it in the glass.

Queen's Park Swizzle

TropicalHerbalCitrusSpicyWoodyGrassy
  • Aged Rum60 ml
  • Fresh Lime Juice30 ml
  • Simple Syrup22 ml
  • Mint Leaves8 leaves
  • Angostura Bitters2 dashes

Preparation

1. Gently Muddle: In a highball glass, gently muddle the mint leaves with the simple syrup. Be careful not to over-muddle, as this can release bitter compounds. 2. Add Ingredients: Add the aged rum, lime juice, and Angostura bitters to the glass. 3. Fill with Crushed Ice: Fill the glass completely with crushed ice. 4. Swizzle: Insert a swizzle stick (or a long spoon) into the glass and swizzle vigorously, using a back-and-forth motion. The goal is to chill and aerate the drink simultaneously, creating a frosty effect. Continue swizzling until the glass is frosted on the outside. 5. Add More Ice: Add more crushed ice to form a cone above the rim of the glass. 6. Garnish: Garnish with a generous sprig of fresh mint. Optionally, dust the mint sprig with powdered sugar.

Garnish

Mint sprig, dusted with powdered sugar (optional)

Cement Mixer

SourCreamyGrassy
  • Lime Juice20 ml

Preparation

Pour the milk into a shot glass. Carefully layer the lime juice on top, avoiding mixing as much as possible. Serve immediately and instruct the drinker to consume the shot quickly.

None

Whiskey Smash

FruityCitrusHerbalWoodyGrassy
  • Bourbon60 ml
  • Mint Leaves8 leaves
  • Simple Syrup15 ml
  • Lemon Wedges30 ml

Preparation

1. In a shaker, gently muddle the mint leaves, lemon wedges, and simple syrup. Avoid over-muddling the mint, which can make it bitter. 2. Add the bourbon to the shaker. 3. Fill the shaker with ice. 4. Shake well until chilled. 5. Double strain into an Old Fashioned glass filled with crushed ice. 6. Garnish with a fresh mint sprig and a citrus wedge.

Garnish

Mint sprig and a citrus wedge or wheel (e.g., lemon, orange)

Gin Fizz

CitrusHerbalGrassyFloral
  • Gin45 ml
  • Lemon Juice30 ml
  • Simple Syrup15 ml
  • Soda Water90 ml

Preparation

1. Chill a highball glass. 2. In a shaker, combine gin, lemon juice, and simple syrup. 3. Add ice to the shaker. 4. Shake well until chilled. 5. Strain into the chilled highball glass filled with ice. 6. Top with soda water. 7. Gently stir to combine. 8. Garnish with a lemon wheel or wedge.

Garnish

Lemon wheel or wedge

Virgin Mojito

CitrusHerbalTropicalFruityGrassy
  • Fresh Mint Leaves12 leaves
  • Lime Juice30 ml
  • Simple Syrup20 ml
  • Soda Water120 ml
  • Lime Wedges4 wedges

Preparation

1. Gently muddle the mint leaves, lime wedges, and simple syrup in a sturdy glass. Avoid over-muddling, as this can release bitter compounds from the mint. 2. Add the lime juice. 3. Fill the glass with ice. 4. Top with soda water. 5. Stir gently to combine. 6. Garnish with a mint sprig and a lime wedge.

Garnish

Mint sprig and lime wedge. Gently slap the mint sprig to release its aromas before placing it on the drink.

Sake Bomb

UmamiYeastyGrassy
  • Sake45 ml
  • Beer355 ml

Preparation

1. Fill a pint glass about 3/4 full with beer. 2. Balance a pair of chopsticks horizontally across the top of the glass. 3. Place a shot glass containing sake on top of the chopsticks, balancing it in the middle. 4. Coordinate a shout (e.g., 'Sake Bomb!') with your companions, then sharply strike the table, causing the chopsticks to separate and the shot of sake to fall into the beer. 5. Immediately consume the mixture.

Garnish

None
