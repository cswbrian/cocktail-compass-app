10:T4cf,The Aviation is a classic cocktail that emerged in the early 20th century, created by Hugo Ensslin, the head bartender at the Hotel Wallick in New York. Its name is derived from the light blue hue imparted by crème de violette, evoking the color of the sky. The original recipe called for gin, lemon juice, maraschino liqueur, and crème de violette. The crème de violette, however, can be difficult to find, and its floral note can be overpowering. As a result, many modern versions omit or reduce the amount of crème de violette, leading to a simpler, drier cocktail. Takumi's Aviation aims for balance, celebrating the violet flavor but grounding it with gin and acidity. Finding that precise balance between floral sweetness, tartness, and dry gin is key to a well-made Aviation. Some bartenders have used the butterfly pea flower to give the cocktail a purple tint without the violet flavor. The cocktail's balance is delicate; too much violette dominates, while too little makes it a tart gin sour. The Aviation represents a time of optimism and innovation and remains a sophisticated and beautiful drink. Different gins will affect the flavor profile, so try different ones.
