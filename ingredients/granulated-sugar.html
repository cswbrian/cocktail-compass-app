Cocktails with Granulated Sugar

Glögg

FruitySpicyWarmSweetWoody
  • Red Wine250 ml
  • Brandy50 ml
  • Cinnamon Sticks2 pc
  • Whole Cloves10 pc
  • Cardamom Pods10 pc
  • Raisins25 g
  • Blanched Almonds25 g
  • Granulated Sugar25 g
  • Orange Peel1 strip

Preparation

Combine red wine, brandy, cinnamon sticks, cloves, cardamom pods, raisins, blanched almonds, sugar, and orange peel in a large pot. Heat gently over low heat, ensuring the mixture does not boil. Simmer for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld. Strain the Glögg to remove the spices and orange peel. Serve warm in mugs, garnishing each serving with a fresh orange peel and a few raisins and almonds.

Garnish

Orange peel and a few raisins and blanched almonds in each serving mug

Quentão

SpicyFruityCitrusWoodySweet
  • Cachaça500 ml
  • Water200 ml
  • Granulated Sugar200 g
  • Fresh Ginger50 g
  • Cinnamon Sticks3 pc
  • Whole Cloves6 pc
  • Orange1 pc
  • Lime1 pc

Preparation

1. Slice the ginger into thin coins. Cut the orange and lime into quarters. 2. In a large pot, combine the water, sugar, ginger, cinnamon sticks, and cloves. 3. Bring the mixture to a boil over medium heat, stirring until the sugar is dissolved. 4. Reduce the heat to low and add the orange and lime pieces. Simmer for about 15-20 minutes to allow the flavors to infuse. 5. Stir in the Cachaça. 6. Heat gently for another 5 minutes, being careful not to boil off the alcohol. (The mixture is supposed to be gently heated). 7. Strain the mixture to remove the solids. 8. Serve hot in mugs or heat-resistant glasses. Garnish as specified.

Garnish

Orange slices studded with cloves and cinnamon sticks.

Cremat

CitrusSpicyFruityNuttyWoodySmoky
  • Rum200 ml
  • Granulated Sugar100 g
  • Lemon Peel1 pc
  • Orange Peel1 pc
  • Coffee Beans5 pc
  • Cinnamon Stick1 pc

Preparation

1. In a heat-resistant earthenware bowl or pot, combine the rum, sugar, lemon peel, orange peel, coffee beans, and cinnamon stick. 2. Stir the mixture gently to dissolve some of the sugar. 3. Carefully heat the rum mixture, either on a stovetop or using a heat source under the bowl. Ensure it doesn't boil. 4. Using a ladle, scoop some of the heated rum and pour it back over the sugar and spices. This helps to dissolve the sugar further and infuse the flavors. 5. Once the sugar is mostly dissolved, carefully ignite the surface of the rum with a long match or lighter. The alcohol fumes will catch fire and burn with a blue flame. 6. Allow the rum to burn for a few minutes, stirring occasionally with a ladle. The longer it burns, the more alcohol evaporates, reducing the overall booziness. 7. Extinguish the flames by covering the bowl with a lid or by carefully adding a small amount of water or coffee. 8. Ladle the Cremat into heat-resistant glasses or mugs, ensuring that each serving contains a portion of the citrus peels and coffee beans. 9. Serve immediately and enjoy warm.

Garnish

Lemon peel, orange peel, coffee beans (3-5), caramelized sugar around the rim (optional)
