Cocktails with Amaretto

Godfather

NuttyWoodySweetSmoky
  • Scotch Whisky50 ml
  • Amaretto25 ml

Preparation

Pour Scotch whisky and amaretto into an Old Fashioned glass filled with ice. Stir gently to combine. Garnish as desired.

Garnish

Optional: Lemon or orange twist. Express oils over the drink if used.

Sweetness: 6

Sourness: 1

Body: 7

Complexity: 6

Booziness: 7

French Connection

NuttyVanillaWoodyFruity
  • Cognac60 ml
  • Amaretto30 ml

Preparation

Pour cognac and amaretto into a mixing glass with ice. Stir gently until well-chilled. Strain into a rocks glass with or without ice, according to preference.

Garnish

None typically, but an optional lemon twist can add a touch of citrus aroma.

Sweetness: 7

Sourness: 1

Body: 6

Complexity: 7

Booziness: 6

Blueberry Tea

NuttyFruityCitrusSweetWarm
  • Amaretto15 ml
  • Orange Liqueur15 ml
  • Hot Water90 ml

Preparation

Combine amaretto and orange liqueur in a mug. Top with hot water. Stir gently to combine. Garnish with an orange slice or lemon twist.

Garnish

Orange slice or lemon twist

Sourness: 2

Body: 4

Complexity: 5

Booziness: 2

Amaretto Sour

FruityCitrusNuttyVanillaSweet
  • Amaretto60 ml
  • Fresh Lemon Juice30 ml
  • Egg White15 ml
  • Angostura Bitters2 dashes

Preparation

1. If using egg white, dry shake all ingredients (without ice) vigorously for 15 seconds to emulsify the egg white. 2. Add ice to the shaker. 3. Shake well until chilled. 4. Double strain into an ice-filled rocks glass.

Garnish

Lemon peel and a maraschino cherry. Express the lemon peel over the drink to release oils, then place it on the rim or float on top, along with the cherry.

Sweetness: 8

Sourness: 6

Body: 6

Complexity: 7

Booziness: 5

Quick Fuck

SweetCreamyCoffeeNutty

  • Irish Cream15 ml
  • Amaretto15 ml

Preparation

Carefully layer the ingredients in a shot glass in the following order: coffee liqueur, Irish cream, and then amaretto. Pour slowly over the back of a spoon to maintain distinct layers.

None

Flaming Doctor Pepper

NuttySweetWarmAlmond
  • Amaretto22 ml
  • High-Proof Rum (151 proof or higher)22 ml
  • Beer (Light Lager)150 ml

Preparation

1. Pour the beer into a heat-resistant glass, leaving about an inch of space at the top. 2. In a separate heat-resistant shot glass, carefully layer the amaretto. 3. Float the high-proof rum on top of the amaretto in the shot glass. 4. Carefully ignite the rum in the shot glass using a long lighter or match. 5. Allow the flame to burn for a few seconds (no more than 5-10 seconds). 6. Extinguish the flame by dropping the shot glass into the beer-filled glass, be careful to use a bar spoon to avoid splashing. 7. Consume immediately and responsibly.

Garnish

None recommended due to the nature of the drink. Safety is the priority.

Sweetness: 8

Sourness: 1

Body: 4

Complexity: 5

Booziness: 7
