Cocktails with Fresh Mint Leaves

Mint Julep

HerbalSweetWoodyEarthyVanilla
  • Bourbon Whiskey60 ml
  • Fresh Mint Leaves10 leaves
  • Simple Syrup10 ml

Preparation

1. Gently muddle mint leaves and simple syrup in a julep cup or sturdy rocks glass. Avoid over-muddling, which can release bitter compounds. 2. Fill the cup completely with crushed ice. 3. Add bourbon. 4. Gently stir until the cup is frosted. 5. Add more crushed ice to form a mound. 6. Garnish with a generous sprig of fresh mint. 7. Optionally, dust with powdered sugar.

Garnish

A generous sprig of fresh mint, lightly slapped to release its aroma. Optionally, dust with powdered sugar.

Sweetness: 6

Sourness: 1

Body: 5

Complexity: 6

Booziness: 6

Virgin Mojito

CitrusHerbalTropicalFruityGrassy
  • Fresh Mint Leaves12 leaves
  • Lime Juice30 ml
  • Simple Syrup20 ml
  • Soda Water120 ml
  • Lime Wedges4 wedges

Preparation

1. Gently muddle the mint leaves, lime wedges, and simple syrup in a sturdy glass. Avoid over-muddling, as this can release bitter compounds from the mint. 2. Add the lime juice. 3. Fill the glass with ice. 4. Top with soda water. 5. Stir gently to combine. 6. Garnish with a mint sprig and a lime wedge.

Garnish

Mint sprig and lime wedge. Gently slap the mint sprig to release its aromas before placing it on the drink.

Sweetness: 5

Sourness: 7

Body: 4

Complexity: 6

Booziness: 0

Cojito

FruityCitrusHerbalTropicalCreamy
  • White Rum60 ml
  • Coconut Cream40 ml
  • Fresh Mint Leaves10 leaves
  • Lime Juice30 ml
  • Simple Syrup20 ml
  • Soda Water60 ml

Preparation

1. Gently muddle mint leaves with simple syrup in a sturdy glass or shaker. Avoid over-muddling to prevent bitterness. 2. Add white rum, lime juice, and coconut cream to the glass. 3. Fill the glass with ice. 4. Top with soda water. 5. Gently stir to combine all ingredients. 6. Garnish with a mint sprig and a lime wedge.

Garnish

Mint sprig and a lime wedge

Sweetness: 7

Sourness: 6

Body: 6

Complexity: 7

Booziness: 4
