Cocktail Compass

Americano

Ingredients

  • Campari1 oz
  • Sweet Vermouth1 oz
  • Soda Water3 oz

Garnish: Orange peel twist, expressed over the drink and dropped in.

Instructions

Fill a rocks glass with ice. Add Campari and sweet vermouth. Top with soda water. Gently stir to combine. Garnish with an orange peel twist.

Flavour Profile

Sweetness: 4

Sourness: 3

Body: 4

Complexity: 6

Booziness: 2

The Americano is a classic aperitivo cocktail that originated in Italy in the 1860s. It's believed to have been named after the Italian word for 'bitter,' 'amaro,' or perhaps due to its popularity among American tourists visiting Italy. It predates the Negroni and can be seen as its lighter, less boozy ancestor. The cocktail’s simple composition highlights the interplay between the bitter Campari, the sweet vermouth, and the refreshing soda water. It's a perfect choice for warm weather and is typically enjoyed before a meal to stimulate the appetite. The combination of Campari and sweet vermouth creates a complex flavor profile with herbal, citrus, and subtly spicy notes, while the soda water adds a pleasant effervescence. The Americano remains a popular choice in bars around the world for its refreshing quality and ease of preparation, embodying the simplicity and elegance of Italian cocktail culture. Its low alcohol content makes it an ideal choice for those seeking a lighter drink, and it’s a great option for daytime sipping. The Americano is often considered a sophisticated and refreshing alternative to stronger cocktails, appealing to a wide range of palates. Its enduring popularity demonstrates its timeless appeal as a simple, flavorful, and balanced drink. It is typically garnished with an orange peel, however a lemon peel can also be used.
