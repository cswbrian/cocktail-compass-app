Cocktail Compass

Boulevardier

Ingredients

  • Bourbon45 ml
  • Campari30 ml
  • Sweet Vermouth30 ml

Garnish: Orange peel twist, expressed over the drink

Instructions

Combine all ingredients in a mixing glass filled with ice. Stir until well-chilled (approximately 20-30 seconds). Strain into a chilled coupe or rocks glass over a large ice cube. Express the oils from an orange peel over the drink and garnish with the peel.

Flavour Profile

Sweetness: 5

Sourness: 3

Body: 7

Complexity: 8

Booziness: 7

The Boulevardier is a classic cocktail that is essentially a whiskey-based Negroni. It is believed to have originated in the 1920s at Harry's New York Bar in Paris, a popular watering hole for American expatriates. The drink is named after the magazine 'Boulevardier,' which was published in Paris and founded by Erskine Gwynne, an American socialite. The cocktail is a simple yet elegant combination of whiskey (typically bourbon or rye), sweet vermouth, and Campari. Its equal-parts structure makes it easy to remember and replicate. The Boulevardier offers a balance of bitter, sweet, and spirituous flavors, making it a sophisticated choice for whiskey lovers. The whiskey provides a warming base, the sweet vermouth adds depth and sweetness, and the Campari contributes a distinct bitter edge. This trifecta of flavors makes the Boulevardier a complex and intriguing drink that is both refreshing and satisfying. Compared to its more famous cousin, the Negroni, the Boulevardier swaps gin for whiskey, creating a richer and more intense experience.
