Cocktail Compass

Boulevardier

Flavor Notes

BitterHerbalFruityWoody

Ingredients

  • Bourbon30 ml
  • Campari30 ml
  • Sweet Vermouth30 ml

Garnish: Orange peel twist (expressed and inserted)

Instructions

Combine all ingredients in a mixing glass filled with ice. Stir until well-chilled (approximately 20-30 seconds). Strain into a chilled coupe or rocks glass over a large ice cube.

Flavour Profile

Sweetness: 4

Sourness: 3

Body: 7

Complexity: 8

Booziness: 7

The Boulevardier is a classic cocktail that's essentially a Negroni with whiskey swapped in for gin. It's a spirit-forward drink that offers a delightful balance of bitter, sweet, and boozy flavors. Its origins can be traced back to the 1920s in Paris, and it was popularized by Erskine Gwynne, an American expatriate who founded a magazine called Boulevardier. The drink quickly gained recognition for its sophisticated yet simple profile, making it a favorite among cocktail enthusiasts. Its relatively straightforward preparation and the availability of its ingredients have contributed to its enduring popularity. The Boulevardier offers a richer and deeper flavor compared to the Negroni, making it a perfect choice for whiskey lovers. Its versatility also allows for variations using different types of whiskey or adjusting the proportions to suit individual preferences. It is an excellent cocktail for cooler evenings or when a more robust and complex drink is desired. The Boulevardier stands as a testament to the enduring appeal of well-balanced and thoughtfully crafted cocktails that have stood the test of time.
