Gin Sour

Flavor Notes

CitrusHerbalSourSweet

Ingredients

  • Gin60 ml
  • Lemon Juice30 ml
  • Simple Syrup22 ml
  • Egg White15 ml

Garnish: Lemon peel twist or a few drops of bitters on the egg white foam (if used). Express the oils from the lemon peel over the drink before placing it on the rim.

Instructions

1. If using egg white, combine all ingredients in a cocktail shaker without ice (dry shake) for 15 seconds to emulsify the egg white. 2. Add ice to the shaker and shake vigorously for another 15-20 seconds to chill and further emulsify the mixture. 3. Double strain into a chilled coupe or rocks glass. If serving in a rocks glass, fill with fresh ice. 4. Garnish with a lemon peel twist or a few drops of bitters on the egg white foam (if used).

The Gin Sour is a classic cocktail that balances the botanical notes of gin with the tartness of lemon juice and the sweetness of sugar. The addition of egg white, while optional, creates a silky smooth texture and a frothy head, elevating the drink's overall experience. Its origins can be traced back to the early days of cocktail making, evolving from simple punches to more refined concoctions. The Gin Sour is part of the larger 'sour' family of cocktails, characterized by a base spirit, citrus, and sweetener. The Gin Sour gained popularity during the late 19th and early 20th centuries, appearing in numerous cocktail books and becoming a staple in bars worldwide. It's a versatile drink that can be tailored to individual preferences by adjusting the sweetness or sourness levels. The quality of the gin significantly impacts the final flavor profile; London Dry gins are a common choice, providing a clean and crisp base, while more contemporary gins with unique botanical blends can add a modern twist. The Gin Sour remains a beloved choice for both seasoned cocktail enthusiasts and newcomers, appreciated for its simplicity, balance, and refreshing qualities. Its enduring popularity is a testament to its timeless appeal and the versatility of its basic formula.
