Lynchburg Lemonade

Flavor Notes

CitrusFruitySweet

Ingredients

  • Whiskey40 ml
  • Triple Sec20 ml
  • Lemon Juice30 ml
  • Simple Syrup20 ml
  • Lemon-Lime Soda120 ml

Garnish: Lemon wedge

Instructions

1. Fill a highball glass with ice. 2. Add whiskey, triple sec, lemon juice, and simple syrup. 3. Top with lemon-lime soda. 4. Stir gently to combine. 5. Garnish with a lemon wedge.

The Lynchburg Lemonade is a refreshing and deceptively potent cocktail that gained popularity in the 1980s. It is named after Lynchburg, Tennessee, home of the Jack Daniel's distillery. The cocktail's creation is somewhat debated, with various stories attributing its origin to different bartenders in the area. Regardless of its exact genesis, the Lynchburg Lemonade quickly became a signature drink associated with the distillery and the town. The cocktail's appeal lies in its balance of sweet, sour, and boozy flavors, making it a crowd-pleaser, especially during warm weather. Its simple construction and readily available ingredients have contributed to its widespread adoption in bars and home settings. While variations exist, the classic recipe features whiskey, triple sec, lemon juice, and simple syrup, topped with lemon-lime soda for effervescence and a touch of sweetness. The drink's popularity has led to the Jack Daniel's company officially endorsing and marketing a version of it, further solidifying its place in cocktail culture. Its bright, citrusy profile and easy-drinking nature make it a favorite for casual gatherings and backyard barbecues. This drink is sure to please!
