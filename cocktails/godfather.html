Cocktail Compass

Godfather

Ingredients

  • Scotch Whisky50 ml
  • Amaretto25 ml

Garnish: Optional: Orange zest twist, expressed over the glass to release oils, then discarded or placed on the rim.

Instructions

Pour Scotch whisky and amaretto into an old-fashioned glass filled with ice cubes. Stir gently to combine and chill. Optionally garnish.

Flavour Profile

Sweetness: 5

Sourness: 1

Body: 7

Complexity: 6

Booziness: 8

The Godfather cocktail is a simple yet elegant drink that combines the rich flavors of Scotch whisky with the sweet almond notes of amaretto. Its origin is debated, with some claiming it was a favorite of Marlon Brando, hence the name. The combination creates a smooth, subtly sweet, and intensely flavored experience. It is typically served in an old-fashioned glass with ice. The simplicity of the recipe allows for easy preparation and appreciation of the high-quality ingredients. The Godfather is often enjoyed as an after-dinner drink due to its strong flavor profile and warming effect. The balance between the smoky, oaky notes of the Scotch and the sweet, nutty character of the amaretto is key to a well-made Godfather. This drink is a testament to the idea that sometimes, less is more. The precise proportions may vary to suit individual preferences, with some opting for a slightly sweeter or stronger drink by adjusting the amount of amaretto or Scotch. While no verifiable historical documentation exists to confirm its direct association with Marlon Brando, the connection remains a compelling part of the cocktail's lore. It stands as a classic example of how a few ingredients, expertly combined, can create a memorable and timeless drink.
