Cocktail Compass

Japanese Slipper

Ingredients

  • Midori Melon Liqueur30 ml
  • Cointreau30 ml
  • Lemon Juice30 ml

Garnish: A maraschino cherry, placed on a cocktail pick or directly into the glass.

Instructions

Chill a coupe or martini glass. Add Midori, Cointreau, and lemon juice to a cocktail shaker filled with ice. Shake well until chilled. Strain into the chilled glass.

Flavour Profile

Sweetness: 8

Sourness: 5

Body: 4

Complexity: 5

Booziness: 4

The Japanese Slipper is a vibrant and visually appealing cocktail known for its sweet and fruity flavor profile. Created in 1984 by Jean-Paul Bourguignon at Mietta's Restaurant in Melbourne, Australia, it showcases the harmonious blend of Midori melon liqueur, Cointreau, and lemon juice. This combination results in a refreshing and well-balanced drink that's easy to enjoy. The cocktail's bright green color, derived from the Midori, contributes to its popularity, making it a favorite for those who appreciate both taste and presentation. The drink gained popularity in the 1980s and is still a well-known cocktail today. Its simple recipe makes it easy to recreate at home, while its sophisticated flavors make it a suitable choice for cocktail parties or a quiet evening. The Japanese Slipper represents a playful yet refined approach to cocktail making, inviting drinkers to indulge in a touch of sweetness and zest. The use of Midori, a relatively modern liqueur compared to others, highlights the cocktail's contemporary roots and appeal. The cocktail's name evokes a sense of exoticism, adding to its allure. Overall, the Japanese Slipper is a testament to the creativity and innovation in cocktail culture, offering a delightful experience for the palate and the eyes.
