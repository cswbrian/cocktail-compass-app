Cocktail Compass

Vesper

Ingredients

  • Gin60 ml
  • Vodka15 ml
  • Lillet Blanc7 ml

Garnish: Large, thin slice of lemon peel, expressed over the drink and placed in the glass.

Instructions

Combine gin, vodka, and Lillet Blanc in a cocktail shaker filled with ice. Shake very well until the shaker is thoroughly chilled. Strain into a chilled cocktail glass. Express the oils from a large, thin slice of lemon peel over the drink and drop the peel into the glass.

Flavour Profile

Sweetness: 2

Sourness: 3

Body: 6

Complexity: 7

Booziness: 9

The Vesper, famously ordered by James Bond in Ian Fleming's 1953 novel 'Casino Royale', is a sophisticated and potent cocktail that combines gin, vodka, and a French aromatized wine. Bond's specific instructions called for "Three measures of Gordon's, one of vodka, half a measure of Kina Lillet. Shake it very well until it's ice-cold, then add a large thin slice of lemon peel." The cocktail is named after Vesper Lynd, a double agent whom Bond is enamored with in the novel. While Kina Lillet is no longer produced, it is often substituted with Lillet Blanc, though the original Kina Lillet was more bitter. Modern variations sometimes include a dash of quinine to mimic the original bitterness. The Vesper stands out from the Martini due to its use of both gin and vodka, along with Lillet, resulting in a complex interplay of botanicals and subtle sweetness. Due to its high alcohol content and minimal dilution, the Vesper is a strong cocktail, best suited for those who appreciate a dry and assertive drink. The lemon peel garnish provides a bright, aromatic counterpoint to the spirit-forward base, contributing to the cocktail's overall balance. Its creation reflects Bond's penchant for meticulously crafted drinks and adds to the character's aura of sophistication and discerning taste.
