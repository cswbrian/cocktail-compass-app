Its creation is often attributed to Robert "Rosebud" Butt in 1972 at the Oak Beach Inn on Long Island, New York. Butt concocted the drink as an entry in a contest to create a new mixed drink using triple sec. The drink quickly gained popularity due to its deceivingly sweet taste, which masks its high alcohol content. The combination of multiple spirits makes it a strong drink, while the addition of cola gives it the appearance of iced tea. Today, the Long Island Iced Tea is a popular choice in bars and restaurants worldwide, known for its strength and its ability to deliver a considerable alcoholic punch. Variations of the drink exist, but the core components of vodka, gin, rum, tequila, triple sec, and a sour mix remain consistent. The Long Island Iced Tea is a testament to the art of mixology, where seemingly disparate ingredients can come together to create a uniquely palatable and potent beverage. It is often enjoyed during social gatherings, parties, and casual nights out. The drink's popularity has solidified its place as a modern classic in the world of cocktails.
