Cocktail Compass

Paloma

Flavor Notes

CitrusFruityBitterSaltyTropical

Ingredients

  • Tequila Blanco60 ml
  • Grapefruit Soda90 ml
  • Lime Juice15 ml

Garnish: Grapefruit wedge and salt rim (optional). Cut a grapefruit wedge and rub it along the rim of the glass. Then, roll the rim in coarse salt before adding ice and the cocktail.

Instructions

Prepare the glass by rimming it with salt, if desired. Fill a highball glass with ice. Add Tequila Blanco and Lime Juice, then top with Grapefruit Soda. Stir gently to combine. Garnish with a grapefruit wedge.

The Paloma is a refreshing and simple cocktail that hails from Mexico, offering a delightful balance of sweetness, tartness, and a hint of bitterness. While its exact origins are debated, it's widely believed to have been created in the town of Tequila, Mexico, sometime in the 1950s. Some attribute it to Don Javier Delgado Corona, the legendary owner and bartender of La Capilla, a famous bar in Tequila, though concrete evidence remains elusive. The name 'Paloma,' meaning 'dove' in Spanish, adds to the drink's light and airy feel. Traditionally, the Paloma is made with tequila, grapefruit soda (such as Jarritos or Squirt), and lime juice, served over ice with a salt rim. The grapefruit soda provides both sweetness and a distinctive citrusy tang, while the lime juice adds a necessary sour counterpoint. The salt enhances the overall flavor profile, accentuating the sweetness and mitigating any perceived bitterness from the grapefruit. While the Paloma is often overshadowed by its more famous cousin, the Margarita, it is exceptionally popular in Mexico, offering a less sweet and more nuanced alternative. Bartenders are now experimenting with fresh grapefruit juice and agave nectar to provide a sophisticated spin on the classic recipe. Today, the Paloma is enjoyed worldwide as a refreshing and easy-to-make cocktail, perfect for warm weather and casual gatherings. It represents the essence of Mexican hospitality and the vibrant flavors of its culture.
