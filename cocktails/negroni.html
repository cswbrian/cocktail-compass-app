Cocktail Compass

Negroni

Ingredients

  • Gin30 ml
  • Campari30 ml
  • Sweet Vermouth30 ml
  • None0 ml

Garnish: Orange peel, expressed and placed in the glass.

Instructions

1. Add gin, Campari, and sweet vermouth to a mixing glass filled with ice. 2. Stir well until chilled, approximately 20-30 seconds. 3. Strain into a rocks glass filled with ice. 4. Garnish with an orange peel, expressed over the glass and placed inside.

Flavour Profile

Sweetness: 5

Sourness: 4

Body: 6

Complexity: 8

Booziness: 7

The Negroni is a classic Italian cocktail, known for its simple yet bold combination of gin, Campari, and sweet vermouth. Its history is traced back to Florence, Italy, in the early 20th century. Legend has it that Count Camillo Negroni, seeking a stronger version of the Americano, requested the soda water be replaced with gin. This substitution gave birth to the Negroni, quickly becoming a popular aperitivo. The equal parts recipe makes it easy to remember and replicate, contributing to its enduring appeal. The drink's vibrant red color and bittersweet taste are instantly recognizable. It's typically served on the rocks and garnished with an orange peel, which adds a citrusy aroma and enhances the overall flavor profile. The Negroni is more than just a drink; it's a cultural icon, embodying the Italian spirit of simplicity, quality, and balance. It's a favorite among cocktail enthusiasts for its assertive flavor and its ability to stimulate the appetite before a meal. The equal parts construction allows for experimentation with different gins and vermouths, leading to endless variations on this timeless classic. Its enduring popularity is a testament to the power of a well-balanced and flavorful cocktail.
