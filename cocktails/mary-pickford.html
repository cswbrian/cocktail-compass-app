Cocktail Compass

Mary Pickford

Flavor Notes

FruityTropicalSweetCitrus

Ingredients

  • White Rum45 ml
  • Maraschino Liqueur5 ml
  • Grenadine5 ml
  • Pineapple Juice45 ml

Garnish: Maraschino cherry, preferably Luxardo or similar quality.

Instructions

Chill a coupe or martini glass. Combine all ingredients in a cocktail shaker with ice. Shake well until chilled. Double strain into the chilled glass.

Flavour Profile

Sweetness: 7

Sourness: 4

Body: 5

Complexity: 6

Booziness: 3

The Mary Pickford cocktail, named after the famous silent film actress, is a delightful and refreshing drink that emerged during the Prohibition era. It's believed to have originated in Havana, Cuba, where Mary Pickford was filming a movie. The drink quickly gained popularity due to its balanced sweetness, slight tartness, and tropical notes. The combination of white rum, pineapple juice, grenadine, and maraschino liqueur creates a harmonious blend of flavors that is both easy to drink and sophisticated. Its elegant presentation, often garnished with a maraschino cherry, adds to its allure. The Mary Pickford cocktail captures the glamour and spirit of the Roaring Twenties, offering a taste of classic Hollywood charm. It is a simple yet elegant cocktail that reflects the era and persona of its namesake. Its enduring appeal is a testament to its well-balanced flavor profile and its connection to a celebrated figure of early cinema. The cocktail remains a popular choice for those seeking a taste of vintage glamour and a perfectly balanced, fruity libation.
