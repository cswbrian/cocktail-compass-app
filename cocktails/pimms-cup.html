Cocktail Compass

Pimm's Cup

Ingredients

  • Pimm's No. 150 ml
  • Lemonade or Ginger Ale150 ml
  • Cucumber3 slices
  • Orange2 slices
  • Strawberry3 halves
  • Mint5 leaves

Garnish: Cucumber ribbons, orange slices, strawberry halves, mint sprigs. The preparation involves slicing cucumber into thin ribbons, halving strawberries, slicing oranges, and gently slapping mint sprigs to release aroma.

Instructions

Fill a highball glass with ice. Add Pimm's No. 1. Top with lemonade or ginger ale. Gently stir to combine. Garnish with cucumber ribbons, orange slices, strawberry halves, and mint sprigs.

Flavour Profile

Sweetness: 5

Sourness: 3

Body: 4

Complexity: 7

Booziness: 2

The Pimm's Cup is a quintessential British summer cocktail, celebrated for its refreshing and complex flavor profile. Invented by James Pimm in the 1840s, it was originally served as a digestive tonic in his oyster bars in London. Pimm's No. 1, the base of the drink, is a gin-based liqueur infused with herbs, spices, and citrus. The exact recipe remains a closely guarded secret, contributing to the drink's unique character. Over time, the Pimm's Cup evolved from a simple digestif to a social staple, particularly at garden parties, sporting events like Wimbledon, and summer gatherings. Its appeal lies in its versatility and ease of preparation; it's typically lengthened with lemonade or ginger ale and garnished with an array of fruits and vegetables, creating a visually appealing and palate-pleasing beverage. Each variation of the Pimm's Cup reflects the personal preferences and seasonal availability of ingredients, making it a truly adaptable and enjoyable drink. The combination of the herbal Pimm's, crisp cucumber, sweet fruits, and bubbly mixer creates a balanced and invigorating experience. Pimm's Cup is considered a low ABV drink that is easy to drink and enjoy for extended periods. Its widespread popularity has cemented its place as a beloved summer tradition in the UK and beyond.
