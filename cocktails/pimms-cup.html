Cocktail Compass

Pimm's Cup

Flavor Notes

FruityCitrusHerbalSpicyBitter

Ingredients

  • Pimm's No. 150 ml
  • Lemonade150 ml
  • Cucumber2 slices
  • Orange2 slices
  • Strawberry2 halves
  • Mint5 leaves

Garnish: Cucumber ribbons, orange slices, strawberry halves, and a sprig of mint.

Instructions

Fill a highball glass or jug with ice. Add Pimm's No. 1. Top with lemonade. Gently stir to combine. Garnish with cucumber ribbons, orange slices, strawberry halves, and a sprig of mint.

Flavour Profile

Sweetness: 5

Sourness: 3

Body: 4

Complexity: 7

Booziness: 2

The Pimm's Cup is a quintessential British summer cocktail, celebrated for its refreshing and complex flavor profile. Created in the 1840s by James Pimm at his oyster bar in London, Pimm's No. 1 was initially intended as a digestive aid. Over time, it evolved into a popular mixed drink, particularly enjoyed at social events like Wimbledon and garden parties. The exact recipe for Pimm's No. 1 remains a closely guarded secret, but it is known to be gin-based and infused with a blend of herbs, spices, and fruit extracts. A classic Pimm's Cup typically combines Pimm's No. 1 with lemonade (often carbonated), various fresh fruits (such as oranges, strawberries, and cucumber), and herbs like mint or borage. The drink's appeal lies in its balance of sweetness, bitterness, and herbal notes, making it both refreshing and sophisticated. There are many regional variations, including the addition of ginger ale or sparkling wine for extra fizz. Its enduring popularity is a testament to its versatility and its ability to evoke the spirit of British summertime. The Pimm's Cup has become more than just a drink; it's a cultural icon, synonymous with warm weather, leisurely afternoons, and convivial gatherings.
