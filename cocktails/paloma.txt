The name 'Paloma,' meaning 'dove' in Spanish, adds to the drink's light and airy feel. Traditionally, the Paloma is made with tequila, grapefruit soda (such as Jarritos or Squirt), and lime juice, served over ice with a salt rim. The grapefruit soda provides both sweetness and a distinctive citrusy tang, while the lime juice adds a necessary sour counterpoint. The salt enhances the overall flavor profile, accentuating the sweetness and mitigating any perceived bitterness from the grapefruit. While the Paloma is often overshadowed by its more famous cousin, the Margarita, it is exceptionally popular in Mexico, offering a less sweet and more nuanced alternative. Bartenders are now experimenting with fresh grapefruit juice and agave nectar to provide a sophisticated spin on the classic recipe. Today, the Paloma is enjoyed worldwide as a refreshing and easy-to-make cocktail, perfect for warm weather and casual gatherings.
