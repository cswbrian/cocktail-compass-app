Old Pal

Flavor Notes

BitterHerbalSpicyWoody

Ingredients

  • Rye Whiskey45 ml
  • Campari30 ml
  • Dry Vermouth30 ml

Garnish: Lemon or orange peel, expressed and discarded or affixed to the rim; use lemon for dryness or orange for slight sweeteness

Instructions

Combine all ingredients in a mixing glass with ice. Stir until well-chilled, about 15-20 seconds. Strain into a chilled coupe or rocks glass. Garnish with a lemon or orange peel, expressed over the drink and discarded.

The Old Pal is a classic cocktail that elegantly combines the boldness of rye whiskey with the bittersweet notes of dry vermouth and Campari. It's essentially a Boulevardier (which uses sweet vermouth) but with dry vermouth, making it drier and more bitter. The drink's origins are somewhat hazy, but it likely emerged in the early 20th century, a time when bartenders were experimenting with different combinations of spirits and modifiers. The name "Old Pal" suggests a drink meant for companionship and shared enjoyment, and its balanced yet assertive flavor profile makes it a sophisticated choice for those who appreciate a complex and not-too-sweet cocktail.
