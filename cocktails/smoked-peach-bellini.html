Smoked Peach Bellini

Ingredients

  • Prosecco30 ml
  • Peach Liqueur5 ml
  • Smoked Peach Puree50 ml

Garnish: Thin peach slice, gently fanned on the rim of the glass. Optionally, add a small sprig of fresh thyme.

Instructions

1. Smoke fresh peaches using a smoker or by placing peach halves cut-side up in a foil pan and smoking with wood chips for approximately 1-2 hours, depending on the intensity of smoke desired. Cool the peaches. 2. Blend the smoked peaches into a smooth puree. Strain if desired. 3. In a chilled champagne flute, gently pour in the smoked peach puree. 4. Add the peach liqueur. 5. Top with chilled Prosecco, pouring slowly to maintain the bubbles. 6. Gently stir to combine.

Flavour Profile

Sweetness: 7

Sourness: 2

Body: 4

Complexity: 6

Booziness: 3

The Smoked Peach Bellini elevates the classic Bellini with a smoky depth that intertwines beautifully with the sweet and juicy peach. The traditional Bellini, a simple mix of peach puree and Prosecco, was invented at Harry's Bar in Venice, Italy, sometime between 1934 and 1948. Giuseppe Cipriani, the founder of Harry's Bar, named it after the artist Giovanni Bellini, as the drink's pink hue reminded him of Bellini's paintings. This variation introduces a modern twist by smoking the peaches before pureeing them, adding layers of complexity and intrigue to the familiar flavor profile. The smoking process enhances the peach's sweetness and adds an earthy dimension that creates a sophisticated and memorable cocktail. It's a perfect balance of tradition and innovation, ideal for brunches, celebrations, or any occasion that calls for a touch of elegance and unexpected flavor.
