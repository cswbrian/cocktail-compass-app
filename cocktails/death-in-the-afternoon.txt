10:T51d,The Death in the Afternoon, also known as the Hemingway Champagne, is a potent and effervescent cocktail created by Ernest Hemingway. Its simple yet striking combination of absinthe and Champagne delivers a unique sensory experience. The cocktail's invention is attributed to Hemingway, who included the recipe in a collection of celebrity cocktail recipes published in 1935. The name itself reflects Hemingway's characteristic dark humor and appreciation for strong, straightforward drinks. The preparation is exceptionally simple: absinthe is poured into a Champagne flute and then topped with Champagne. The drink should then be stirred gently until it obtains an opalescent cloudiness, creating a visually appealing effect. The result is a drink that is both refreshing and intensely flavored. The anise notes of the absinthe are balanced by the dryness and bubbles of the Champagne, creating a surprisingly harmonious blend. Despite its simplicity, the Death in the Afternoon is not for the faint of heart due to its high alcohol content. It’s best enjoyed slowly, allowing the flavors to fully develop on the palate. This cocktail remains a favorite among those seeking a bold and distinctive drinking experience, embodying Hemingway's adventurous spirit and appreciation for the finer things in
