Cocktail Compass

Mai Tai

Flavor Notes

TropicalNuttyCitrusFruityHerbal

Ingredients

  • Aged Jamaican Rum30 ml
  • Aged Rhum Agricole30 ml
  • Orange Curaçao15 ml
  • Fresh Lime Juice22 ml
  • Orgeat Syrup15 ml

Garnish: Mint sprig and lime wheel, express lime peel over the drink.

Instructions

Add all ingredients to a shaker with ice. Shake well until chilled. Strain into a double rocks glass filled with crushed ice. Garnish as directed.

The Mai Tai is a quintessential tiki cocktail, born in 1944 at Trader Vic's in Oakland, California. Victor Bergeron, aka Trader Vic, created the drink for some Tahitian friends, who exclaimed "Maita'i roa a'e!" which translates to "Out of this world! – the best!" in Tahitian. The original recipe focused on showcasing the flavor of high-quality Jamaican rum, complemented by orgeat syrup, lime juice, curaçao, and simple syrup. Over time, variations emerged, often incorporating other fruit juices like pineapple and orange, which deviate from the original intent. This cocktail is all about balancing the sweetness of the syrups with the tartness of the lime and the robust character of the rums. The interplay of flavors creates a tropical escape in a glass. While its history is relatively modern compared to other classics, the Mai Tai has become an iconic symbol of tiki culture, representing relaxation, exoticism, and escapism. The drink's popularity soared in the mid-20th century, fueled by the rise of Polynesian-themed restaurants and bars. Today, it remains a beloved cocktail, though often misinterpreted and over-sweetened. A well-made Mai Tai should highlight the rum, creating a harmonious blend of flavors that transports you to a tropical paradise.
