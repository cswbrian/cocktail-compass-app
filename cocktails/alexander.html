Cocktail Compass

Alexander

Ingredients

  • Gin30 ml
  • Crème de Cacao30 ml
  • Heavy Cream30 ml

Garnish: Grated nutmeg; sprinkle atop the foam for aromatic spice and visual appeal.

Instructions

Combine all ingredients in a shaker with ice. Shake well until thoroughly chilled. Strain into a chilled coupe or martini glass.

Flavour Profile

Sweetness: 7

Sourness: 1

Body: 8

Complexity: 6

Booziness: 4

The Alexander is a classic cocktail featuring gin, crème de cacao, and cream. Its origins are debated, but one popular theory links it to New York bartender Troy Alexander, who supposedly created it to showcase the then-less-popular crème de cacao. Another theory suggests it was named after Alexander the Great, a nod to its potentially potent and sophisticated nature. Regardless of its true origin, the Alexander gained popularity in the early 20th century, finding mentions in classic cocktail books. It is often considered a dessert cocktail due to its creamy and chocolatey nature, making it a decadent treat. It can be seen as a variation of the Brandy Alexander, which substitutes brandy for gin. Both versions offer a rich, velvety texture and a sweet profile, appealing to those who enjoy creamy and indulgent drinks. While its popularity has waned over the decades, the Alexander remains a well-regarded classic for its simplicity and satisfying flavor.
