Cocktail Compass

Damn the Weather

Flavor Notes

SpicyCitrusHerbalFruityWoody

Ingredients

  • Rye Whiskey45 ml
  • Orange Curaçao7 ml
  • Sweet Vermouth22 ml
  • Angostura Bitters2 dashes

Garnish: Orange peel, expressed over the drink and then discarded

Instructions

Combine all ingredients in a mixing glass filled with ice. Stir well until chilled, approximately 20-30 seconds. Strain into a chilled coupe or Nick & Nora glass.

Flavour Profile

Sweetness: 5

Sourness: 3

Body: 6

Complexity: 8

Booziness: 7

The 'Damn the Weather' cocktail is a modern classic, created by bartender Joaquin Simó at Pouring Ribbons in New York City. It's a delightful mix of rye whiskey, sweet vermouth, curaçao, and aromatic bitters, resulting in a complex yet balanced drink. The cocktail's name is a playful jab at the unpredictability of weather, much like the cocktail's layered flavors that shift and evolve with each sip. Simó's creation showcases the versatility of rye whiskey, complementing its inherent spiciness with the sweetness of vermouth and the citrus notes of curaçao. A dash of bitters ties everything together, adding a touch of bitterness and enhancing the overall aromatic profile. 'Damn the Weather' is a well-balanced and smooth cocktail, making it a great choice for both whiskey enthusiasts and those new to spirit-forward drinks. It's a testament to careful ingredient selection and precise measurements, resulting in a harmonious blend that is both satisfying and memorable. Its enduring popularity is a testament to Simó's skill in crafting a cocktail that is both complex and approachable.
