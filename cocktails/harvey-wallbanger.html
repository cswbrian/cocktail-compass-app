Harvey Wallbanger

Flavor Notes

CitrusVanillaFruityHerbal

Ingredients

  • Vodka45 ml
  • Galliano L'Autentico15 ml
  • Orange Juice90 ml

Garnish: Orange slice

Instructions

Pour vodka and orange juice into a highball glass filled with ice. Gently float the Galliano on top. Garnish with an orange slice.

The Harvey Wallbanger is a variation on the Screwdriver cocktail that became popular in the 1960s and 1970s. Legend has it that the drink was created in 1952 by Donato 'Duke' Antone, a bartender at Duke's Blackwatch Bar in Los Angeles, California. The name supposedly came from a surfer named Harvey who frequented the bar and would bang his head against the wall after consuming a few of these cocktails. The drink's popularity surged thanks to an aggressive marketing campaign featuring a cartoon surfer. Though it experienced a decline in popularity in later years, it remains a classic example of mid-century cocktail culture. It's a simple drink with a distinctive orange and herbal flavor profile that makes it very palatable.
