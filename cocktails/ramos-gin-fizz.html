Ramos Gin Fizz

Ingredients

  • Gin45 ml
  • Orange Flower Water3 drops
  • Heavy Cream30 ml
  • Fresh Lemon Juice15 ml
  • Fresh Lime Juice15 ml
  • Egg White1 pc
  • Soda Water60 ml
  • Sugar2 tsp

Garnish: Orange blossom water spritz and floated edible flowers such as pansies or violets

Instructions

1. Chill a Collins glass. 2. In a shaker, combine gin, heavy cream, lemon juice, lime juice, egg white, orange flower water, and sugar. 3. Dry shake (without ice) vigorously for 30 seconds to emulsify the egg white. 4. Add ice to the shaker and shake again vigorously for at least 1 minute, or until the shaker is very cold. 5. Strain the mixture into the chilled Collins glass. 6. Slowly top with soda water to create a frothy head, pouring in a thin stream. 7. Allow the foam to rise above the rim of the glass. 8. Optional spritz of orange flower water on top. 9. Garnish.

The Ramos Gin Fizz, also known as the New Orleans Fizz, is a classic cocktail renowned for its creamy texture, complex flavors, and elaborate preparation. Created in 1888 by Henry C. Ramos at his bar, the Imperial Cabinet Saloon, in New Orleans, it quickly gained popularity and became a signature drink of the city. The key to a proper Ramos Gin Fizz is the vigorous shaking, traditionally lasting for an extended period, often involving multiple bartenders to share the task. This intense shaking emulsifies the egg white and dairy, creating a frothy and velvety smooth texture. The orange flower water imparts a delicate floral aroma that is characteristic of the drink. The addition of soda water at the end creates a light and effervescent quality. It remains a beloved cocktail, cherished for its unique character and historical significance.
