Aperol Spritz

Flavor Notes

BitterCitrusFruityHerbal

Ingredients

  • Aperol60 ml
  • Prosecco90 ml
  • Soda Water30 ml

Garnish: Orange slice, placed on the rim of the glass.

Instructions

Fill a large wine glass with ice. Add Prosecco, followed by Aperol. Top with soda water. Gently stir to combine.

The Aperol Spritz, a quintessential Italian aperitivo, embodies the spirit of carefree summer evenings and convivial gatherings. Its origins can be traced back to the early 20th century in the Veneto region of Italy, evolving from a simple spritz—a blend of wine and sparkling water introduced by Austrian soldiers during their occupation of the region. In 1919, the Barbieri brothers of Padua created Aperol, a vibrant orange-hued liqueur with a unique bittersweet flavor profile. Aperol quickly gained popularity, and by the 1950s, the Aperol Spritz began to take shape as we know it today, becoming a beloved pre-dinner drink throughout Italy. The recipe was formalized in the 2000s with the 'Spritz Life' campaign, solidifying its place as a global phenomenon. The Aperol Spritz represents the Italian culture of enjoying life's simple pleasures, it pairs perfectly with light snacks or appetizers and evokes a sense of Italian charm. Its low alcohol content makes it a refreshing choice for daytime enjoyment.
