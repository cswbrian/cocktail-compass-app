Cocktail Compass

Bramble

Ingredients

  • Gin50 ml
  • Crème de Mûre15 ml
  • Lemon Juice25 ml
  • Simple Syrup25 ml

Garnish: Fresh blackberries and a lemon wedge.

Instructions

Fill an Old Fashioned glass with crushed ice. Add the gin, lemon juice, and simple syrup. Stir gently to combine. Drizzle the Crème de Mûre over the top of the crushed ice, allowing it to cascade down. Garnish with fresh blackberries and a lemon wedge.

Flavour Profile

Sweetness: 6

Sourness: 7

Body: 4

Complexity: 7

Booziness: 4

The Bramble, created in 1980s London by Dick Bradsell at Fred's Club, is a modern classic celebrated for its balance of sweet, sour, and herbaceous notes. Bradsell's inspiration stemmed from his childhood memories of foraging for blackberries on the Isle of Wight. The cocktail uses dry gin as its base, brightened with fresh lemon juice and sweetened with simple syrup. The crowning touch is a drizzle of crème de mûre (blackberry liqueur), which cascades through the drink, resembling brambles in a thorny bush. The Bramble is a versatile drink, easily adaptable to different gins and fruit liqueurs. Its enduring popularity lies in its simplicity and the way it showcases the interplay between the spirit, citrus, and fruit. It is typically served over crushed ice, which further dilutes the drink and enhances its refreshing qualities, making it an ideal choice for warmer weather or as an aperitif.
