Cocktail Compass

Vieux Carré

Ingredients

  • Rye Whiskey22 ml
  • Cognac22 ml
  • Sweet Vermouth22 ml
  • Bénédictine7 ml
  • Angostura Bitters2 dashes
  • Peychaud's Bitters2 dashes

Garnish: Lemon peel, expressed and placed in the glass, and a cocktail cherry

Instructions

Add all ingredients to a mixing glass filled with ice. Stir until well-chilled, about 20-30 seconds. Strain into an ice-filled old-fashioned glass. Express the oils of a lemon peel over the drink, then drop the peel into the glass. Garnish with a cocktail cherry.

Flavour Profile

Sweetness: 5

Sourness: 3

Body: 7

Complexity: 9

Booziness: 8

The Vieux Carré, meaning 'Old Square' in French, is a classic New Orleans cocktail created in 1938 by Walter Bergeron, head bartender at the Hotel Monteleone's Carousel Bar. This potent and complex drink is a sophisticated combination of rye whiskey, cognac, sweet vermouth, Bénédictine, and both Peychaud's and Angostura bitters. It is stirred, not shaken, and served on the rocks, typically garnished with a lemon twist. The Vieux Carré is considered one of the definitive New Orleans cocktails, embodying the city's rich history and multicultural influences. Its layered flavors offer a balanced interplay of spice, sweetness, bitterness, and herbal notes, making it a favorite among cocktail enthusiasts seeking a refined and spirit-forward experience. The drink's balanced profile of sweet, sour, and bitter elements makes it a great digestif and perfect for the serious cocktail consumer. The combination of spirits and liqueurs creates a unique depth of flavor, making it a memorable and iconic drink representative of New Orleans' cocktail culture. Today, it remains a staple on cocktail menus worldwide, a testament to its enduring appeal and craftsmanship.
