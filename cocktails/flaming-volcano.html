Flaming Volcano

Flavor Notes

TropicalFruitySweetSpicy

Ingredients

  • Light Rum60 ml
  • Dark Rum30 ml
  • Brandy30 ml
  • Orgeat Syrup15 ml
  • Pineapple Juice60 ml
  • Orange Juice30 ml
  • Lime Juice15 ml
  • Sugar Cube1 pc

Garnish: Pineapple wedge, cherry, and a flaming sugar cube soaked in high-proof rum. Ensure sugar cube is placed on a heat-resistant surface within the bowl before ignition.

Instructions

1. Combine light rum, dark rum, brandy, pineapple juice, orange juice, lime juice, and orgeat syrup in a shaker with ice. 2. Shake well until chilled. 3. Strain into a volcano bowl or large serving dish. 4. Soak a sugar cube in high-proof rum (at least 75% ABV). 5. Place the sugar cube on a heat-resistant surface in the center of the bowl. 6. Carefully ignite the sugar cube using a long lighter or match. 7. Garnish with a pineapple wedge and cherry.

The Flaming Volcano is a potent and visually stunning tiki cocktail, typically served in a communal bowl designed to resemble a volcano. Its origins are often attributed to tiki bars of the mid-20th century, like Don the Beachcomber or Trader Vic's, although a definitive origin story remains elusive. The drink is characterized by its blend of multiple rums, brandy, and tropical fruit juices, resulting in a sweet, strong, and complex flavor profile. The 'flaming' aspect is achieved by soaking a sugar cube in high-proof alcohol and setting it alight, creating a dramatic and theatrical presentation. This cocktail is meant for sharing and embodies the spirit of tiki culture: exotic flavors, strong spirits, and a sense of convivial celebration. It's often served with long straws, encouraging guests to partake together in this fiery concoction. The flaming element is a key part of the experience, adding warmth and visual appeal, while the combination of sweet and strong elements makes it a memorable and potent drink. The Flaming Volcano exemplifies the escapism and adventure that tiki cocktails sought to provide.
