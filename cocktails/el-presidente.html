Cocktail Compass

El Presidente

Flavor Notes

FruityCitrusHerbalSweet

Ingredients

  • White Rum45 ml
  • Orange Curaçao7 ml
  • Dry Vermouth15 ml
  • Grenadine1 dash

Garnish: Orange peel, expressed and placed in the glass

Instructions

Chill a coupe or martini glass. Add all ingredients to a mixing glass filled with ice. Stir until well-chilled (approximately 20-30 seconds). Strain into the chilled glass. Express the oils from an orange peel over the drink and place the peel in the glass.

The El Presidente is a classic Cuban cocktail that predates the Cuban Revolution. Its origins are somewhat debated, but it is believed to have been created in honor of a Cuban president, possibly Mario García Menocal, sometime in the early 20th century. The drink gained popularity among American tourists visiting Cuba during the Prohibition era, becoming a staple in Havana bars. The El Presidente is a sophisticated and balanced cocktail. It shares similarities with the Manhattan and Martini families, but with a distinct Cuban flair. The use of white rum distinguishes it, offering a lighter and more vibrant base compared to whiskey or gin. The original recipe likely used French vermouth (dry), but Italian vermouth (sweet) is the modern preference. A dash of grenadine adds a subtle sweetness and a touch of color, while orange curaçao contributes citrus notes and complexity. Some variations use a small amount of simple syrup in place of the grenadine. The cocktail is typically served straight up in a chilled coupe or martini glass and garnished with an orange peel. This cocktail represents a bygone era of Cuban elegance and cocktail innovation.
