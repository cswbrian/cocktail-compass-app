Greenpoint

Flavor Notes

HerbalBitterSpicyWoody

Ingredients

  • Rye Whiskey50 ml
  • Yellow Chartreuse15 ml
  • Sweet Vermouth15 ml
  • Angostura Bitters2 dashes
  • Orange Bitters1 dash

Garnish: Lemon peel, expressed and discarded

Instructions

Stir all ingredients with ice in a mixing glass until well-chilled. Strain into a chilled coupe or Nick & Nora glass. Express the oils from a lemon peel over the drink and discard the peel.

The Greenpoint cocktail is a sophisticated variation on the Manhattan, created in 2006 by Michael McIlroy at the Milk & Honey bar in New York City. Named after the Greenpoint neighborhood in Brooklyn, it substitutes Italian sweet vermouth with Yellow Chartreuse, resulting in a drier and more herbaceous profile. The Greenpoint offers a more complex and nuanced experience compared to the classic Manhattan, appealing to those who appreciate subtle botanical notes and a slightly bitter edge. Its balanced profile makes it a sophisticated choice for both seasoned cocktail enthusiasts and those seeking a refined alternative to sweeter drinks. The interplay between the rye, Chartreuse, and bitters creates a memorable and satisfying drinking experience, showcasing the elegance of well-crafted variations on established classics. The cocktail has gained recognition as a modern classic, demonstrating how small ingredient changes can significantly transform a familiar template.
