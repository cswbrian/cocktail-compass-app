Cocktail Compass

Espresso Martini

Ingredients

  • Vodka50 ml
  • Coffee Liqueur20 ml
  • Freshly Brewed Espresso30 ml
  • Simple Syrup5 ml

Garnish: Three coffee beans, floated on top in a symmetrical pattern. Select high-quality, visually appealing beans. No preparation is needed other than selecting nice looking beans.

Instructions

1. Chill a coupe or martini glass. 2. Combine vodka, freshly brewed espresso, coffee liqueur, and simple syrup in a shaker with ice. 3. Shake vigorously until well-chilled. 4. Double strain into the chilled glass to remove ice shards and create a smooth texture. 5. Garnish with three coffee beans, placed symmetrically on top of the foam.

Flavour Profile

Sweetness: 6

Sourness: 2

Body: 7

Complexity: 6

Booziness: 5

The Espresso Martini, a modern classic, was created in 1983 by Dick Bradsell at Fred's Club in London. Legend has it that a famous model walked into the bar and asked for a drink that would 'wake me up and then f*** me up'. Bradsell, drawing inspiration from a coffee machine nearby, combined vodka, freshly brewed espresso, coffee liqueur, and sugar to create the now-iconic cocktail. The drink quickly gained popularity for its stimulating and indulgent qualities, becoming a staple in cocktail bars worldwide. Its appeal lies in the balance of caffeine, alcohol, and sweetness, providing an energetic yet sophisticated experience. The Espresso Martini is typically served in a chilled coupe or martini glass, garnished with coffee beans for an elegant presentation. Variations include the addition of flavored vodkas, different types of coffee liqueurs, or even a splash of cream for added richness. Despite its relatively recent origins, the Espresso Martini has cemented its place in cocktail history, demonstrating the enduring appeal of coffee-based cocktails and the power of a well-crafted, innovative drink.
