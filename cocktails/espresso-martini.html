Espresso Martini

Flavor Notes

CoffeeChocolateBitterVanillaCreamy

Ingredients

  • Vodka50 ml
  • Coffee Liqueur20 ml
  • Freshly Brewed Espresso30 ml
  • Simple Syrup5 ml

Garnish: 3 coffee beans, floated on top of the crema, arranged artfully

Instructions

1. Chill a martini glass or coupe. 2. Combine vodka, freshly brewed espresso, coffee liqueur, and simple syrup into a cocktail shaker. 3. Add ice to the shaker. 4. Close and shake vigorously for 15-20 seconds to create a frothy crema. 5. Double strain into the chilled glass. 6. Garnish with three coffee beans, floated on top of the crema.

Flavour Profile

Booziness: 5

The Espresso Martini, a modern classic, emerged in London's vibrant bar scene during the 1980s. Credited to the legendary bartender Dick Bradsell, the drink was reportedly created at the Soho Brasserie for a famous model who requested a cocktail that would "wake me up, and then f*** me up". Bradsell, drawing inspiration from a nearby coffee machine and his own penchant for espresso, combined vodka, coffee liqueur, and freshly brewed espresso to create the now-iconic concoction. The Espresso Martini quickly gained popularity for its stimulating and indulgent qualities, becoming a staple in cocktail bars worldwide. Its appeal lies in the harmonious balance of bitter coffee, sweet liqueur, and the kick of vodka, all presented in an elegant and visually appealing manner. The signature crema, achieved through vigorous shaking, adds a luxurious texture and enhances the overall sensory experience. The Espresso Martini embodies the energy and sophistication of the era in which it was created, continuing to captivate cocktail enthusiasts with its unique blend of caffeine and alcohol.
