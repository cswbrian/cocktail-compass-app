Cocktail Compass

The Blenheim

Flavor Notes

CitrusHerbalBitter

Ingredients

  • Gin60 ml
  • Orange Curaçao7 ml
  • Dry Vermouth15 ml
  • Orange Bitters2 dashes

Garnish: Orange twist, expressed over the drink and then placed on the rim.

Instructions

Combine all ingredients in a mixing glass filled with ice. Stir well until chilled, approximately 20-30 seconds. Strain into a chilled coupe or cocktail glass.

Flavour Profile

The Blenheim is a classic gin-based cocktail known for its refreshing and subtly complex flavor profile. It offers a delightful balance of dryness, sweetness, and herbal notes, making it a sophisticated choice for any occasion. Its exact origins are somewhat obscure, though it likely emerged in the early to mid-20th century during the golden age of cocktails. The Blenheim stands out because it showcases gin's versatility, contrasting the spirit with the sweetness of vermouth and the bitter-orange notes of orange curaçao. The addition of orange bitters further enhances the citrus character. The technique is simple, involving stirring the ingredients with ice to chill and dilute, resulting in a smooth, balanced drink. This combination of flavors and its easy preparation have kept The Blenheim a favorite among cocktail enthusiasts. Despite its relative obscurity compared to drinks like the Martini or Manhattan, The Blenheim offers a unique and rewarding drinking experience.
