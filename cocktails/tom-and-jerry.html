Cocktail Compass

Tom and Jerry

Ingredients

  • Dark Rum30 ml
  • Brandy15 ml
  • Tom and Jerry Batter2 tablespoons
  • Hot Milk120 ml
  • Hot Water30 ml

Garnish: Grated nutmeg and a cinnamon stick.

Instructions

In a mug, combine the Tom and Jerry batter, dark rum, and brandy. Top with equal parts hot milk and hot water. Stir gently to combine. Garnish with grated nutmeg and a cinnamon stick.

Flavour Profile

Sweetness: 7

Sourness: 2

Body: 8

Complexity: 7

Booziness: 6

The Tom and Jerry is a warm, comforting, and boozy drink traditionally enjoyed during the winter months, particularly around Christmas. It's essentially a hot, spiced eggnog fortified with rum and brandy, served in a mug. The drink gained popularity in the 19th century, allegedly named after a popular play called "Tom and Jerry, or Life in London", written by Pierce Egan. A key element of the Tom and Jerry is the batter, a concoction of eggs, sugar, spices, and often butter or shortening, which gives the drink its characteristic creamy texture and rich flavor. Preparing the batter from scratch can be time-consuming, involving separating eggs, whipping the whites to stiff peaks, and then combining all ingredients carefully. While pre-made Tom and Jerry batters are commercially available, many enthusiasts prefer the homemade version for its superior taste and control over ingredients. This drink is not only a delightful indulgence but also a piece of cocktail history, evoking a sense of nostalgia and festive cheer.
