Cocktail Compass

Tom and Jerry

Flavor Notes

CreamySpicySweetVanillaNuttyWoody

Ingredients

  • Dark Rum30 ml
  • Cognac15 ml
  • Egg2 whole
  • Sugar20 g
  • Ground Cinnamon0 pinch
  • Ground Nutmeg0 pinch
  • Ground Cloves0 pinch
  • Hot Milk120 ml

Garnish: Freshly grated nutmeg on top

Instructions

1. Separate the egg white and yolk. 2. Whisk the egg white until stiff peaks form. Gradually add half of the sugar while whisking. Set aside. 3. In a separate bowl, whisk the egg yolk with the remaining sugar until light and creamy. Add the cinnamon, nutmeg, and cloves to the yolk mixture and combine. 4. Gently fold the egg white mixture into the yolk mixture until just combined. Be careful not to overmix. 5. In a preheated mug, combine the dark rum and cognac. 6. Add 2-3 tablespoons of the batter to the mug. 7. Top with hot milk, stirring gently to combine. 8. Grate fresh nutmeg on top for garnish.

The Tom and Jerry is a warm, comforting cocktail perfect for cold weather. It's essentially a hot batter mixed with spirits and hot water or milk. The batter is typically made with eggs, sugar, spices (such as cinnamon, nutmeg, and cloves), and sometimes butter or cream. The drink is traditionally served in a mug and garnished with grated nutmeg. Its origins are often attributed to Pierce Egan, a British sports writer, who created it in the 1820s. While Egan may have popularized it, similar hot egg-based drinks existed before. The Tom and Jerry gained immense popularity in the United States, particularly during the 19th and early 20th centuries. Pre-made Tom and Jerry batter mixes were even commercially available, simplifying the preparation process. While its popularity has waned somewhat in recent years, the Tom and Jerry remains a beloved holiday tradition for many families. Its warming spices and creamy texture evoke feelings of nostalgia and festive cheer. The preparation is somewhat involved compared to simpler cocktails, but the resulting drink is a truly special and memorable experience.
