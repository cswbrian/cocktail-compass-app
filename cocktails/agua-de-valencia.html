Agua de Valencia

Flavor Notes

FruityCitrusSweet

Ingredients

  • Gin50 ml
  • Vodka50 ml
  • Simple Syrup25 ml
  • Cava100 ml
  • Fresh Orange Juice150 ml

Garnish: Orange slice or zest, or optionally a few grapes.

Instructions

Combine gin, vodka, orange juice, and simple syrup (if using) in a pitcher. Stir well to combine. Refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or preferably overnight, to allow the flavors to meld. Just before serving, top with chilled cava. Stir gently to combine. Pour into champagne flutes or wine glasses. Garnish with an orange slice or zest.

Agua de Valencia is a refreshing and potent Spanish cocktail originating from Valencia. Its history begins in 1959 at the Cafe Madrid in Valencia, where regulars challenged the owner, Constante Gil, to create something new and exciting. Gil experimented and eventually concocted this blend of orange juice, cava (Spanish sparkling wine), vodka, and gin. Initially, it was exclusively served at the cafe, but its popularity quickly spread throughout Valencia and beyond. Today, it's a staple in many bars and households in Spain, particularly during festive occasions. The drink is typically served in large pitchers or jugs and shared among friends, embodying the convivial spirit of Spanish culture. Its vibrant color and citrusy aroma make it an appealing and festive choice. While the original recipe remains somewhat guarded, the core components are well-known, and many variations exist, with some bartenders adding a touch of simple syrup or other liqueurs to further customize the flavor profile. Be mindful of its high alcohol content and ensure responsible consumption.
