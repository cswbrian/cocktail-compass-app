Cocktail Compass

Mojito

Flavor Notes

CitrusHerbalTropicalSweet

Ingredients

  • White Rum60 ml
  • Mint Leaves12 Leaves
  • Lime Juice30 ml
  • Sugar2 tsp
  • Soda Water60 ml

Garnish: Mint sprig and lime wedge

Instructions

1. Gently muddle the mint leaves with sugar in a sturdy glass. Avoid over-muddling, which can make the mint bitter. 2. Add lime juice and rum to the glass. 3. Fill the glass with ice. 4. Top with soda water. 5. Gently stir to combine. 6. Garnish with a mint sprig and lime wedge.

The Mojito, a classic Cuban cocktail, is a refreshing blend of white rum, mint, lime juice, sugar, and soda water. Its exact origins are somewhat murky, but it's widely believed to have evolved from earlier medicinal drinks in Cuba. One common story suggests it originated with Sir Francis Drake, who supposedly created a similar drink called 'El Draque' using a crude form of rum, mint, lime, and sugar. Over time, El Draque evolved into the Mojito we know today, with the substitution of aguardiente with rum and the addition of soda water. The Mojito gained international popularity thanks to Ernest Hemingway, who frequented La Bodeguita del Medio in Havana and famously praised the bar's Mojitos. The cocktail's balance of sweetness, acidity, and herbal notes makes it an ideal choice for warm weather and casual gatherings. Modern variations often include different fruits or flavored syrups, but the classic Mojito remains a timeless favorite.
