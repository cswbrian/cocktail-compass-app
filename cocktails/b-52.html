Cocktail Compass

B-52

Flavor Notes

CoffeeChocolateCreamyCitrusVanilla

Ingredients

  • Coffee Liqueur20 ml
  • Irish Cream20 ml
  • Orange Liqueur20 ml

Garnish: None typically, can be flamed for visual effect (optional)

Instructions

Carefully layer the ingredients in a shot glass using the back of a spoon to prevent mixing. First, pour the coffee liqueur into the shot glass. Next, gently layer the Irish cream on top of the coffee liqueur by pouring it slowly over the back of a spoon. Finally, layer the orange liqueur on top of the Irish cream, again using the back of a spoon to maintain distinct layers. For an optional flaming effect, float a small amount of high-proof spirit on top of the orange liqueur and ignite it just before serving.

The B-52 is a layered cocktail composed of coffee liqueur, Irish cream, and orange liqueur. When properly prepared, the ingredients remain separated into three distinct layers, creating a visually appealing drink. Its origins are somewhat murky, with claims attributing its creation to various bartenders in different locations. One popular story suggests that it was invented by Peter Fich, a bartender at the Banff Springs Hotel in Alberta, Canada, in 1977. The name is believed to be a reference to the B-52 Stratofortress bomber, which was a symbol of American military might during the Cold War. The cocktail gained popularity in the late 1970s and early 1980s, becoming a staple at bars and nightclubs worldwide. Its sweet and creamy flavor profile makes it a popular choice for after-dinner drinks or for those who enjoy dessert-like cocktails. The B-52 is often served as a shooter, but it can also be enjoyed sipped slowly to appreciate the different layers of flavor. Variations of the B-52 exist, with some bartenders adding a fourth layer of Grand Marnier or other liqueurs to enhance the complexity of the drink.
