Cocktail Compass

Bijou

Ingredients

  • Gin30 ml
  • Green Chartreuse15 ml
  • Sweet Vermouth15 ml

Garnish: Orange peel twist, expressed over the drink and placed in the glass.

Instructions

Chill a coupe or martini glass. Combine gin, Green Chartreuse, and sweet vermouth in a mixing glass with ice. Stir well until chilled, approximately 20 seconds. Strain into the chilled glass. Express the oils from an orange peel over the drink and garnish with the peel.

Flavour Profile

Sweetness: 5

Sourness: 2

Body: 6

Complexity: 8

Booziness: 7

The Bijou cocktail, meaning 'jewel' in French, is a classic drink conceived by Harry Johnson in the late 19th century. Each ingredient was carefully chosen to represent a precious gem: gin for diamond (clear), sweet vermouth for ruby (red), and Chartreuse for emerald (green). The cocktail's flavors are complex and layered, offering a balance of herbal, spicy, and slightly sweet notes. The Bijou experienced a resurgence in popularity in the early 2000s, as bartenders rediscovered and revived many classic cocktail recipes. Its sophisticated flavor profile and historical significance make it a favorite among cocktail enthusiasts who appreciate a well-balanced and intriguing drink. The interplay between the botanical gin, fortified wine, and herbal liqueur offers a unique tasting experience, making it a standout example of classic cocktail craftsmanship. Modern variations sometimes tweak the ratios to adjust the sweetness or bitterness, but the core concept of a balanced, jewel-toned cocktail remains the same. The drink exemplifies the artistry of balancing strong flavors to create a harmonious and memorable libation.
