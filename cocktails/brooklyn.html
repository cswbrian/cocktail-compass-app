Brooklyn

Flavor Notes

BitterFruityHerbalSpicy

Ingredients

  • Rye Whiskey60 ml
  • Maraschino Liqueur7 ml
  • Amer Picon or Cynar/Ramazzotti7 ml
  • Dry Vermouth15 ml

Garnish: Maraschino cherry

Instructions

Combine all ingredients in a mixing glass with ice. Stir well until chilled. Strain into a chilled coupe or martini glass. Garnish with a maraschino cherry.

The Brooklyn cocktail, a pre-Prohibition gem, offers a sophisticated variation on the Manhattan. Its subtle complexity arises from the blend of rye whiskey, dry vermouth, maraschino liqueur, and Amer Picon or a substitute like Cynar or Ramazzotti. While its exact origins are debated, it's believed to have emerged in the early 20th century, possibly named after the New York City borough. The Brooklyn showcases how a few carefully chosen ingredients can transform a classic template into something entirely new, creating a balanced and intriguing drink for the discerning palate. The touch of bitterness from the Amer Picon (or its alternative) distinguishes it from its more famous cousin, the Manhattan. It emphasizes the importance of quality ingredients and the subtle art of balancing flavors to achieve a truly memorable cocktail experience.
