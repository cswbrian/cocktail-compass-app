Scofflaw

Flavor Notes

CitrusFruitySpicyHerbalBitter

Ingredients

  • Rye Whiskey45 ml
  • Dry Vermouth30 ml
  • Lemon Juice22 ml
  • Grenadine15 ml
  • Orange Bitters2 dashes

Garnish: Orange peel twist, expressed over the drink

Instructions

Combine all ingredients in a shaker with ice. Shake well until chilled. Strain into a chilled coupe or cocktail glass. Express the oils from an orange peel over the drink and use as garnish.

The Scofflaw is a Prohibition-era cocktail that emerged as a defiant gesture against those who abstained from alcohol. The term 'scofflaw' was coined in 1924 to describe someone who flouted Prohibition laws, and this cocktail was likely created around the same time to satirize those who obeyed them. The drink gained popularity through its promotion by Parisian bartenders who aimed to appeal to American tourists seeking a taste of rebellion. Its blend of rye whiskey, dry vermouth, lemon juice, grenadine, and orange bitters creates a balance of sweet, sour, and spicy flavors, making it a complex and intriguing drink that has endured through the years. The Scofflaw embodies the rebellious spirit of the Roaring Twenties and remains a popular choice for those seeking a classic cocktail with a historical edge. The drink's enduring appeal lies in its ability to deliver a sophisticated and flavorful experience, reminding us of a time when enjoying a cocktail was an act of defiance.
