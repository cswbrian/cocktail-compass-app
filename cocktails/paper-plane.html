Paper Plane

Flavor Notes

CitrusBitterHerbalFruity

Ingredients

  • Bourbon22 ml
  • Aperol22 ml
  • Amaro Nonino Quintessentia22 ml
  • Fresh Lemon Juice22 ml

Garnish: Orange peel, expressed and discarded or placed on the rim of the glass.

Instructions

Add all ingredients to a shaker with ice. Shake well until chilled. Double strain into a coupe or Nick & Nora glass. Garnish with an orange peel.

The Paper Plane is a modern classic cocktail created in 2008 by Sasha Petraske and Sam Ross at Milk & Honey in New York City. It was specifically designed for the opening of The Violet Hour in Chicago. This equal-parts cocktail, named after M.I.A.'s hit song 'Paper Planes,' balances the boldness of bourbon with the bitterness of Amaro Nonino, the citrusy tang of lemon juice, and the herbal sweetness of Aperol. The result is a complex, yet refreshing drink that appeals to a wide range of palates. The equal proportion of ingredients makes it easy to remember and replicate, contributing to its widespread popularity in the craft cocktail scene. Its unique flavor profile and balanced structure have cemented its place as a modern classic, frequently found on cocktail menus worldwide. The Paper Plane's success lies in its harmonious blend of contrasting flavors, making it a standout example of contemporary mixology. It's a testament to the enduring appeal of well-balanced, thoughtfully crafted cocktails.
