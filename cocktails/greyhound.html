Greyhound

Flavor Notes

FruityCitrusTart

Ingredients

  • Vodka60 ml
  • Grapefruit Juice120 ml

Garnish: Grapefruit wedge or slice (optional)

Instructions

Fill a highball glass with ice. Add vodka. Top with grapefruit juice. Stir gently to combine. Garnish with a grapefruit wedge, if desired.

The Greyhound is a simple and refreshing cocktail consisting of vodka and grapefruit juice. Its origins are somewhat murky, with some suggesting it originated in the 1930s. The name 'Greyhound' is believed to be derived from the Greyhound bus lines, as the drink was often served in bus terminals and diners along the bus routes. Its simplicity has made it a popular choice over the decades, appreciated for its ease of preparation and thirst-quenching qualities. It‚Äôs a staple on many bar menus due to its straightforward appeal. The cocktail remains a classic choice for those who prefer a tart and subtly sweet beverage without overwhelming complexity. There are many variations. Adding a salted rim transforms it into a Salty Dog, but the core combination of vodka and grapefruit remains the same for the basic Greyhound. The grapefruit can vary from white to pink. The choice of vodka is very personal, as it can change the flavor profile quite a bit. Cheaper vodkas will have a rubbing alcohol taste to them while the higher end vodkas will have very little flavor.
