Cocktail Compass

Clover Club

Flavor Notes

FruityCitrusFloralSourSweet

Ingredients

  • Gin45 ml
  • Raspberry Syrup15 ml
  • Lemon Juice22 ml
  • Egg White15 ml

Garnish: Three fresh raspberries, skewered.

Instructions

1. Combine all ingredients in a cocktail shaker without ice (dry shake). 2. Shake vigorously for 15-20 seconds to emulsify the egg white. 3. Add ice to the shaker. 4. Shake again until well-chilled (about 15 seconds). 5. Double strain into a chilled coupe glass.

Flavour Profile

Sweetness: 5

Sourness: 7

Body: 5

Complexity: 7

Booziness: 4

The Clover Club is a classic pre-Prohibition cocktail that originated at the Bellevue-Stratford Hotel in Philadelphia. It was named after the Clover Club Society, a group of lawyers, writers, and other professionals who met in the hotel's bar. The drink gained popularity in the late 19th and early 20th centuries and was known for its delicate balance of sweet, sour, and slightly tart flavors, along with its distinctive foamy texture from the egg white. While the Clover Club's popularity waned after Prohibition, it has experienced a resurgence in recent years thanks to the renewed interest in classic cocktails. Modern mixologists appreciate its elegant simplicity and the opportunity to showcase high-quality ingredients. The cocktail represents a piece of cocktail history, a time when drinks were crafted with fresh ingredients and attention to detail. Its balanced profile and attractive appearance make it a favorite among those who appreciate sophisticated and flavorful concoctions. The Clover Club is a testament to the enduring appeal of well-made cocktails that stand the test of time, offering a glimpse into the past while remaining relevant in the present.
