Irish Coffee

Flavor Notes

CoffeeVanillaCreamyNuttyWoodySpicy

Ingredients

  • Irish Whiskey50 ml
  • Hot Coffee120 ml
  • Brown Sugar1 tsp
  • Lightly Whipped Cream50 ml

Garnish: Light grating of nutmeg on top of the cream.

Instructions

1. Warm a heatproof glass (such as a stemmed Irish coffee glass) by filling it with hot water, then discard the water. 2. Add brown sugar to the warmed glass. 3. Pour in hot coffee and stir until the sugar is completely dissolved. 4. Add Irish whiskey and stir gently to combine. 5. Gently pour lightly whipped cream over the back of a spoon, allowing it to float on top of the coffee. Do not stir. 6. Garnish with a grating of fresh nutmeg.

Irish Coffee is a classic hot cocktail that combines Irish whiskey, hot coffee, sugar, and a layer of cream. Its invention is often credited to Joe Sheridan, a head chef at Foynes Port near Limerick, Ireland, in 1943. Legend has it that Sheridan added whiskey to the coffee of cold and weary passengers on a transatlantic flight that had to return to Foynes due to bad weather. When asked if it was Brazilian coffee, Sheridan jokingly replied that it was 'Irish coffee.' The drink quickly gained popularity, especially after travel writer Stanton Delaplane introduced it to the Buena Vista Cafe in San Francisco in 1952, which then worked to perfect the recipe. Irish Coffee offers a warming and comforting experience, blending the robustness of whiskey with the rich flavor of coffee and the sweetness of sugar, all topped with a velvety layer of cream.
