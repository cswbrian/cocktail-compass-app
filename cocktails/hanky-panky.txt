Coleman was one of the few female bartenders of her time, and her creation quickly gained popularity among patrons of the Savoy. The name 'Hanky Panky' supposedly came from Coleman's exclamation upon tasting the finished cocktail, declaring it 'the real hanky-panky!' The drink is stirred, not shaken, to maintain its smooth texture, and served in a chilled cocktail glass. The interplay of the gin, vermouth, and Fernet-Branca creates a unique flavor profile, balancing the botanical notes of the gin with the sweet, slightly spicy vermouth and the intensely bitter and herbaceous Fernet-Branca.
