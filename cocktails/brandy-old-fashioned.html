Brandy Old Fashioned

Flavor Notes

FruitySpicyWoodyVanilla

Ingredients

  • Brandy60 ml
  • Sugar Cube1 pc
  • Angostura Bitters2 dashes
  • Water30 ml

Garnish: Orange peel and/or Maraschino cherry. Express the oils from the orange peel over the drink and optionally add the cherry.

Instructions

Place sugar cube in an old fashioned glass. Saturate with Angostura bitters. Add water. Muddle until sugar is dissolved. Fill the glass with ice. Add brandy. Stir gently to combine. Garnish.

The Brandy Old Fashioned is a variation of the classic Old Fashioned cocktail, substituting whiskey with brandy as the base spirit. Originating in the 19th century, the Old Fashioned is considered one of the original cocktails, highlighting the spirit with sugar, bitters, and water. The brandy version gained popularity in the Midwest, particularly in Wisconsin, where brandy production and consumption have a strong tradition. In Wisconsin, it's common to specify the drink 'sweet,' which includes soda; 'sour,' with lemon-lime soda; or 'press,' which uses both club soda and lemon-lime soda. A proper Brandy Old Fashioned showcases the quality of the brandy, complemented by the sweetness and aromatic bitters. The addition of fruit as a muddled component is a popular variation, although purists may argue against it. Whether enjoyed with or without fruit, the Brandy Old Fashioned delivers a balanced and flavorful experience, highlighting the richness and complexity of brandy. It is a testament to the adaptability of classic cocktails and regional preferences in cocktail culture. The simplicity of the drink allows for variations catering to individual tastes, making it a beloved and enduring choice.
