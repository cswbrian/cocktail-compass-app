Cosmopolitan

Flavor Notes

FruityCitrusSour

Ingredients

  • Vodka45 ml
  • Orange Liqueur (e.g., Cointreau)15 ml
  • Cranberry Juice30 ml
  • Lime Juice15 ml

Garnish: Orange peel, expressed and placed on the rim.

Instructions

Chill a coupe or martini glass. Combine vodka, cranberry juice, lime juice, and orange liqueur in a cocktail shaker filled with ice. Shake well until chilled. Double strain into the chilled glass. Express the oils from an orange peel over the drink and place the peel on the rim to garnish.

The Cosmopolitan, often shortened to "Cosmo," is a classic cocktail known for its vibrant pink hue and refreshing taste. Though its exact origins are debated, the modern version is widely attributed to Cheryl Cook at the Strand Restaurant in South Beach, Florida, in the mid-1980s. Cook's intention was to create a visually appealing drink served in a Martini glass that was more palatable than the overly sweet drinks of the time. Toby Cecchini, of The Odeon in New York City, is also often credited with popularizing a version of the Cosmo. However, the cocktail gained massive popularity in the 1990s, largely due to its frequent appearance on the television show "Sex and the City," where it became the signature drink of the character Carrie Bradshaw and her friends. The Cosmo's appeal lies in its balance of sweet, tart, and slightly citrusy flavors, making it a sophisticated and approachable choice for cocktail enthusiasts. Its simple recipe and readily available ingredients have further cemented its status as a modern classic, frequently found on cocktail menus worldwide. The drink is not too strong, and perfect for a celebration or a special treat.
