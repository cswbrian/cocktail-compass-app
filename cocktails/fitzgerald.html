Fitzgerald

Flavor Notes

CitrusHerbalBitter

Ingredients

  • Gin60 ml
  • Lemon Juice30 ml
  • Simple Syrup15 ml
  • Angostura Bitters2 dashes

Garnish: Lemon peel twist (expressed)

Instructions

Chill a coupe or cocktail glass. Combine all ingredients in a shaker with ice. Shake well until chilled. Double strain into the chilled glass. Express the oils from a lemon peel over the drink and use as garnish.

The Fitzgerald is a delightful variation of the classic Gin Sour, distinguished by the addition of a few dashes of Angostura bitters. It offers a refreshing and aromatic profile, balancing sweetness, tartness, and a subtle bitterness. While the exact origins of the Fitzgerald are debated, it is widely attributed to Dale DeGroff, a prominent figure in the cocktail renaissance. DeGroff is known for his meticulous approach to cocktail crafting and his dedication to using fresh, high-quality ingredients. His contributions have significantly influenced modern mixology. The drink embodies a bright and zesty character, making it a fantastic choice for warm weather or as an aperitif. The Angostura bitters add a layer of depth and complexity, differentiating it from a standard gin sour. This cocktail appeals to those who appreciate well-balanced drinks with a touch of sophistication. The Fitzgerald is simple to make, requiring only a few readily available ingredients, making it easy to recreate at home. Its vibrant flavor profile ensures it is a memorable and enjoyable drink, worthy of its place in the contemporary cocktail canon.
