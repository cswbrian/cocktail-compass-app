Martinez

Flavor Notes

FruityHerbalBitterFloral

Ingredients

  • Old Tom Gin45 ml
  • Maraschino Liqueur7 ml
  • Sweet Vermouth20 ml
  • Angostura Bitters2 dashes

Garnish: Orange peel, expressed and placed in the glass or a cocktail cherry.

Instructions

Stir all ingredients with ice in a mixing glass until well-chilled. Strain into a chilled coupe or martini glass.

The Martinez is often considered a precursor to the Martini. It dates back to the late 19th century, with various origin stories. One popular account suggests it was created in Martinez, California, during the Gold Rush era. Another story attributes its creation to a bartender in New York City. Regardless of its precise origin, the Martinez was documented in early bartending guides like O.H. Byron's "The Modern Bartender's Guide" (1884) and Harry Johnson's "Bartender's Manual" (1888). These recipes varied but generally included Old Tom Gin, sweet vermouth, maraschino liqueur, and bitters. Over time, the recipe has evolved, with some modern variations incorporating orange bitters and a lemon twist instead of the traditional olive. The Martinez represents a bridge between sweeter, more approachable cocktails of the 19th century and the drier, more spirit-forward drinks of the 20th century. It offers a complex interplay of flavors, balancing the sweetness of the gin and vermouth with the bitterness of the bitters and the subtle fruitiness of the maraschino liqueur.
