Dry Martini

Flavor Notes

BitterHerbalFloral

Ingredients

  • Gin75 ml
  • Dry Vermouth15 ml

Garnish: Olives (Spanish or Cocktail) or Lemon Twist, depending on preference. For olives, use 1-3 skewered. For lemon twist, express oils over drink and drop in or discard.

Instructions

1. Chill a Martini glass. 2. In a mixing glass filled with ice, combine gin and dry vermouth. 3. Stir gently for 20-30 seconds until well-chilled. 4. Strain into the chilled Martini glass. 5. Garnish with olives or a lemon twist.

The Dry Martini, a quintessential classic cocktail, embodies elegance and simplicity. Its origins are somewhat hazy, with various stories placing its invention in the late 19th century. One popular theory suggests it evolved from the Martinez, a sweeter cocktail served in Martinez, California. Another claims it was created at the Knickerbocker Hotel in New York City. Regardless of its precise origin, the Martini gained prominence during the early 20th century, becoming a symbol of sophistication and a favorite of literary and film icons. Over time, the Martini has undergone numerous variations, with the 'Dry' Martini, characterized by a minimal amount of vermouth, becoming increasingly popular. The ratio of gin to vermouth has steadily shifted, reflecting changing tastes. A very dry Martini may contain only a whisper of vermouth, or none at all, sometimes just a rinse of the glass. This cocktail has captured the imaginations of bartenders and drinkers alike, inspiring countless riffs and personal preferences, but at its core it remains a testament to the power of simplicity and high-quality ingredients. The Martini is more than just a drink; it is a cultural icon, a symbol of timeless style and refined taste.
