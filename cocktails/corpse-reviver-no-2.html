Cocktail Compass

Corpse Reviver No. 2

Ingredients

  • Gin22 ml
  • Lillet Blanc22 ml
  • Cointreau22 ml
  • Absinthe1 dash
  • Lemon Juice22 ml

Garnish: Lemon peel twist (expressed and discarded)

Instructions

Chill a coupe glass. Add all ingredients to a cocktail shaker with ice. Shake well until thoroughly chilled. Double strain into the chilled coupe glass. Express the oils from a lemon peel over the drink and discard the peel.

Flavour Profile

Sweetness: 4

Sourness: 7

Body: 5

Complexity: 8

Booziness: 6

The Corpse Reviver No. 2 is a classic cocktail created to alleviate the effects of overindulgence. It gained prominence in the early 20th century, particularly in cocktail books like Harry Craddock's 'Savoy Cocktail Book' (1930). The cocktail is known for its complex flavor profile, combining floral, citrus, and herbal notes. It was intended as a 'hair of the dog' remedy, meant to be consumed in the morning to revive the drinker after a night of heavy drinking. Its potent combination of spirits and liqueurs provides a strong kick, while the lemon juice and other ingredients offer a refreshing and palatable experience. Over the years, the Corpse Reviver No. 2 has seen numerous variations, but the original recipe remains a popular choice among cocktail enthusiasts for its balanced and invigorating qualities. Today it stands as a testament to the inventive and often whimsical nature of early cocktail culture, with its evocative name and potent effects continuing to captivate drinkers.
