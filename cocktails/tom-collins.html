Cocktail Compass

Tom Collins

Flavor Notes

CitrusFruityHerbalSweetSour

Ingredients

  • Gin50 ml
  • Lemon Juice25 ml
  • Simple Syrup25 ml
  • Club Soda100 ml

Garnish: Lemon wheel and a maraschino cherry

Instructions

In a Collins glass filled with ice, combine gin, lemon juice, and simple syrup. Stir well to combine. Top with club soda. Gently stir again to incorporate the soda. Garnish with a lemon wheel and a maraschino cherry.

Flavour Profile

Complexity: 5

Booziness: 3

The Tom Collins is a classic gin-based cocktail that embodies refreshment and balance. Its origins are somewhat murky, with several stories vying for its creation. One popular narrative traces the drink back to a London head waiter named John Collins, working in the 1820s at Limmer's Hotel. It's said that he crafted a refreshing gin punch that became a favorite among patrons and was later named after him. Another anecdote attributes the drink's creation to a bartender of the same name, who worked at a hotel in New York in the 1870s. Whatever its true origins, the Tom Collins gained immense popularity in the late 19th century and has remained a beloved classic ever since. The drink became incredibly popular in 1874 when it was mentioned in a hoax that spread across New York, blaming a 'Tom Collins' for spreading malicious rumours.
