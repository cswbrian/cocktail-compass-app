Cocktail Compass

Flip

Ingredients

  • Brandy60 ml
  • Sherry10 ml
  • Whole Egg1 pc
  • Sugar20 ml

Garnish: Grated nutmeg; freshly grate over the top of the drink after pouring. A cinnamon stick can also be used as an option

Instructions

Combine brandy, sugar, sherry and egg in a shaker. Dry-shake vigorously without ice for 15 seconds to emulsify the egg. Add ice and shake again until well-chilled. Double strain into a chilled glass. Garnish with grated nutmeg.

Flavour Profile

Sweetness: 6

Sourness: 2

Body: 7

Complexity: 6

Booziness: 6

The Flip is a classic cocktail, historically served warm, dating back to 17th-century England. Originally, it consisted of beer, rum, and sugar, heated with a hot iron rod—the 'flip-dog'—which imparted a burnt, caramel-like flavor. Over time, the Flip evolved, with beer being replaced by wine or spirits and the hot iron by other heating methods or serving it cold. Egg and spices were later additions, transforming it into a richer, more dessert-like drink. Today, it is commonly made with a spirit (often brandy or rum), sugar, and an egg, shaken vigorously to create a frothy texture. Its warmth and spice make it a popular choice during colder months. The Flip represents a fascinating transformation in cocktail history, showcasing how a simple, utilitarian drink evolved into a complex and indulgent treat. The balance of spirit, sweetness, and the creamy texture of the egg make the Flip a truly unique and satisfying cocktail experience.
