Smash

Flavor Notes

FruityHerbalCitrusWoodySweetSour

Ingredients

  • Bourbon60 ml
  • Mint Leaves8 leaves
  • Simple Syrup15 ml
  • Lemon Juice30 ml
  • Fresh Fruit (Berries or Peach)50 g

Garnish: Mint sprig and a fresh fruit slice (e.g., lemon wheel, berry, or peach slice depending on the fruit used in the drink). Gently slap the mint sprig to release its aroma before placing it on the drink.

Instructions

In a shaker tin, gently muddle the mint leaves and fresh fruit with the simple syrup. Add the bourbon and lemon juice. Fill the shaker with ice. Shake well until chilled. Double strain into a rocks glass filled with ice.

The Smash is a versatile and refreshing cocktail that falls into the 'cobbler' family of drinks. It's essentially a julep variation that embraces seasonal fruits and herbs. Originating in the 19th century, the Smash was popularized by legendary bartenders like Jerry Thomas, who included a recipe in his seminal book, 'How to Mix Drinks' (1862). The beauty of the Smash lies in its adaptability; the base spirit can be customized, and the fresh ingredients vary according to what's available. Mint is a common element, but other herbs and fruits, like berries, peaches, and cucumbers, can also be incorporated. The drink is typically built in the glass, allowing the ingredients to meld together through muddling and dilution from ice. The final result is a vibrant, aromatic, and balanced beverage that is perfect for warm weather gatherings.
