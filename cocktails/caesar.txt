10:T473,The Caesar is a distinctly Canadian cocktail, born in Calgary, Alberta in 1969. Created by bartender Walter Chell at the Calgary Inn (now the Westin Calgary) to celebrate the opening of a new Italian restaurant, the Caesar was inspired by the flavors of Spaghetti alle vongole. Chell mixed vodka with clam and tomato juice, Worcestershire sauce, and spices, creating a savory and spicy drink that quickly gained popularity. The key ingredient, Clamato juice (a blend of clam broth and tomato juice), is what sets it apart from the Bloody Mary. While the Bloody Mary enjoys global recognition, the Caesar remains primarily a Canadian favorite, a testament to its unique flavor profile and cultural significance. Variations abound, with bartenders adding their own twists and garnishes, but the core components remain consistent: vodka, Clamato, Worcestershire, hot sauce, and a celery salt rim. The Caesar's enduring popularity is a reflection of its savory, spicy, and refreshing qualities, making it a perfect brunch or anytime drink.
