Cocktail Compass

Horse's Neck

Flavor Notes

CitrusSpicyWoodyVanilla

Ingredients

  • Bourbon Whiskey44 ml
  • Ginger Ale120 ml
  • Angostura bitters3 dashes

Garnish: Long spiral of lemon peel, draped over the rim of the glass.

Instructions

1. Prepare the lemon spiral by cutting a long, continuous strip of peel from the lemon, starting at the top and working your way around. 2. Drape the lemon spiral over the rim of a highball glass, with the majority of the peel hanging outside the glass. 3. Fill the glass with ice cubes. 4. Add Bourbon whiskey to the glass. 5. Add Angostura bitters. 6. Top with ginger ale. 7. Gently stir to combine. 8. Serve immediately.

Flavour Profile

Sweetness: 5

Sourness: 3

Body: 4

Complexity: 3

Booziness: 3

The Horse's Neck is a simple, refreshing highball cocktail that has its roots in the late 19th century. Initially a non-alcoholic drink consisting of ginger ale and a long spiral of lemon peel draped over the edge of the glass (resembling a horse's neck), it gained alcoholic appeal with the addition of bourbon whiskey. It is a very simple cocktail, so it is imperative to use quality ingredients. The ginger ale provides a sweet and spicy base, which is accentuated by the aromatic oils released from the lemon twist. Adding bourbon gives the drink a warm, boozy kick, transforming it from a refreshing cooler into a proper cocktail. While simple, the Horse's Neck highlights how a few well-chosen ingredients can create a balanced and enjoyable drink. It's a fantastic option for those who prefer lighter, less sweet cocktails, and can be easily adapted to different spirits for varied flavor profiles. The Horse's Neck is perfect as an introductory Bourbon experience, as the ginger ale is a complement to the spice and caramel notes.
