Cocktail Compass

Sazerac

Ingredients

  • Rye Whiskey60 ml
  • Absinthe7 ml
  • Sugar Cube1 pc
  • Peychaud's Bitters3 dashes

Garnish: Lemon peel twist, expressed and discarded. Essential oils are expressed over the drink.

Instructions

Chill an Old Fashioned glass. In a separate mixing glass, muddle the sugar cube with the Peychaud's bitters. Add the rye whiskey and ice. Stir well until chilled. Coat the chilled Old Fashioned glass with absinthe, swirling to coat the inside, then discard the excess. Strain the whiskey mixture into the absinthe-rinsed glass. Express the oil from a lemon peel over the glass, then discard the peel.

Flavour Profile

Sweetness: 3

Sourness: 2

Body: 7

Complexity: 9

Booziness: 8

The Sazerac is a New Orleans classic, often considered one of the oldest American cocktails. Its history is intertwined with the Sazerac Coffee House, where it was first popularized in the mid-19th century. Originally made with Sazerac de Forge et Fils cognac, the cocktail transitioned to rye whiskey as phylloxera devastated French vineyards. The drink's unique flavor profile comes from the combination of rye whiskey, Peychaud's bitters, absinthe (or a substitute), and a touch of sugar. The ritual of coating the glass with absinthe before adding the other ingredients is crucial, imparting a distinct aroma and flavor that complements the other components. A lemon peel twist, expressed over the drink but not dropped in, adds a final layer of citrus aroma and essential oils, enhancing the overall complexity. The Sazerac stands as a testament to New Orleans' rich cocktail heritage, showcasing a balance of spice, bitterness, and subtle sweetness.
