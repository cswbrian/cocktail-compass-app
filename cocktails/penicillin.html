Penicillin

Flavor Notes

SmokySpicyCitrusHerbalSweetEarthy

Ingredients

  • Blended Scotch Whisky60 ml
  • Islay Single Malt Scotch Whisky7 ml
  • Fresh Lemon Juice22 ml
  • Honey-Ginger Syrup22 ml

Garnish: Candied ginger and/or a lemon peel

Instructions

Muddle ginger slices (if using fresh ginger), add blended Scotch, lemon juice, and honey-ginger syrup to a shaker. Add ice and shake well until chilled. Strain into a rocks glass filled with ice. Float the Islay Scotch on top. Garnish with candied ginger and/or a lemon peel.

The Penicillin is a modern classic cocktail created in 2005 by Sam Ross at Milk & Honey in New York City. It's a variation on the Gold Rush, a whiskey sour made with honey syrup. Ross's Penicillin distinguishes itself by adding ginger and a float of smoky Islay Scotch. The cocktail is a delightful balance of sweet, sour, and spicy flavors, with a pronounced smoky aroma. The ginger adds a warm, spicy kick that complements the honey's sweetness, while the lemon juice provides a refreshing tartness. The Islay Scotch, typically Laphroaig, adds a layer of complexity with its distinctive peaty and medicinal notes. The Penicillin quickly gained popularity for its innovative flavor combination and has become a staple in many cocktail bars worldwide. It is often cited as one of the most influential cocktails of the 21st century. The drink's name suggests its supposed medicinal properties, hinting at the soothing qualities of honey, ginger, and whisky, though it is mostly a playful reference. It's a sophisticated and balanced cocktail suitable for both whisky enthusiasts and those looking for a more adventurous drink.
