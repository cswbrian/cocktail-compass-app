Cocktail Compass

Moscow Mule

Flavor Notes

SpicyCitrusRefreshing

Ingredients

  • Vodka60 ml
  • Ginger Beer90 ml
  • Lime Juice15 ml

Garnish: Lime wedge (fresh)

Instructions

Fill a copper mug with ice. Add vodka and lime juice. Top with ginger beer. Gently stir to combine. Garnish with a lime wedge.

Flavour Profile

Sweetness: 4

Sourness: 6

Body: 4

Complexity: 5

Booziness: 4

The Moscow Mule is a classic cocktail known for its refreshing and spicy flavor profile. It gained popularity in the mid-20th century, largely due to clever marketing and the distinctive copper mug in which it is traditionally served. The cocktail's creation is often attributed to John G. Martin of Heublein (now Diageo) and Jack Morgan, owner of the Cock 'n' Bull restaurant in Los Angeles, around 1941. Facing difficulties in selling their Smirnoff vodka and ginger beer respectively, they combined the two ingredients and added a squeeze of lime. The copper mug was introduced to further enhance the drink's appeal. The Moscow Mule quickly became a sensation, especially in Hollywood, and helped popularize vodka in the United States. Its simplicity and refreshing nature have ensured its lasting appeal, making it a staple in bars around the world. The combination of vodka, ginger beer, and lime creates a balanced and easy-to-drink cocktail that is perfect for warm weather or any occasion.
