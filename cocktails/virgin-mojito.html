Cocktail Compass

Virgin Mojito

Flavor Notes

CitrusHerbalTropicalFruityGrassy

Ingredients

  • Fresh Mint Leaves12 leaves
  • Lime Juice30 ml
  • Simple Syrup20 ml
  • Soda Water120 ml
  • Lime Wedges4 wedges

Garnish: Mint sprig and lime wedge. Gently slap the mint sprig to release its aromas before placing it on the drink.

Instructions

1. Gently muddle the mint leaves, lime wedges, and simple syrup in a sturdy glass. Avoid over-muddling, as this can release bitter compounds from the mint. 2. Add the lime juice. 3. Fill the glass with ice. 4. Top with soda water. 5. Stir gently to combine. 6. Garnish with a mint sprig and a lime wedge.

Flavour Profile

Sweetness: 5

Sourness: 7

Body: 4

Complexity: 6

Booziness: 0

The Virgin Mojito is a non-alcoholic adaptation of the classic Mojito cocktail. Its history parallels that of the original Mojito, which is believed to have originated in Havana, Cuba. The exact origins are murky, but it's thought to have evolved from a medicinal drink. Sir Francis Drake, a famous English sea captain and privateer, is sometimes associated with an early version of the Mojito, as his crew mixed aguardiente (a crude form of rum), mint, lime, and sugar to combat scurvy and dysentery. The modern Mojito, made with rum, gained popularity in the early 20th century, particularly at the Havana bar La Bodeguita del Medio, famously frequented by Ernest Hemingway. The Virgin Mojito emerged later, as a response to the growing demand for sophisticated non-alcoholic drinks. It retains the refreshing qualities of the original, relying on the vibrant flavors of mint, lime, and sparkling water to create a balanced and invigorating beverage. The Virgin Mojito provides all the refreshment without the alcohol.
